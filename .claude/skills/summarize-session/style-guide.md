# ClaudeBook Writing Style Guide

Reference this file when generating ClaudeBook narrative chapters with `/summarize-session`.

## Voice & Tense
- Third-person past tense throughout
- Active voice preferred: "Mirr drew her blade" not "A blade was drawn by Mirr"
- Named characters by name, not "the party" when possible

## Structure (3 paragraphs minimum)

**Opening** — Establish where and when. Ground the reader in the scene before action begins.
> *The settlement of Thornhaven was quiet that morning, its muddy streets empty as dawn crept over the Veiled Peaks...*

**Rising Action** — Trace the session's main events in narrative order. Each scene transition gets its own sentence or short paragraph. Maintain cause-and-effect logic.

**Closing** — End with the unresolved tension, cliffhanger, or emotional beat that sends readers to the next chapter.
> *Pipe's trail went cold at the edge of the forest. Whatever waited in those trees, it would have to wait one more night.*

## What Makes a Good Chapter
- Specific sensory detail (one per scene is enough)
- Character voice surfacing through action, not narration ("Kass studied the symbol. She'd seen it before.")
- Consequences feel earned — reference earlier sessions if relevant
- Cliffhanger earns the next chapter's `←` link

## What to Cut
- Dice rolls and game mechanics entirely
- "The party decided to..." (replace with specific characters doing specific things)
- Meta-commentary ("In this session...")
- Failed attempts that didn't matter to the story

## Wiki-Link Guidelines
- Link character names on first mention per chapter: `[[Mirr]]`
- Link location names on first mention: `[[Veiled Peaks]]`
- Link items when plot-relevant: `[[Blood root|Blood Root]]`
- Do NOT link every occurrence — once per chapter per entity is enough

## Length Target
300–500 words. Shorter is better if the session was light. Never pad.
