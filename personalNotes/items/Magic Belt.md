# Hero's Belt - DM Notes

> *"Only a true hero can wear it."*

## The Object

A wide leather belt reinforced with bronze rivets and a heavy iron buckle engraved with a mountain peak split by lightning. The leather is old but supple — clearly Goliath-made, sized for a large frame. Where other belts show wear, this one shows use: scuff marks from battle, not decoration.

The inscription on the inside of the belt, in Giant script: **"Worth is not claimed. It is carried."**

## The Secret

The belt does not decide who is worthy — it reveals who already is. It cannot be forced open by the unworthy; the buckle simply will not catch. It has rejected lords, generals, and champions of renown. It sat unclaimed for decades because no one *needed* it.

It chose [[Mirr]] the moment she touched it — not because she is the most powerful person nearby, but because she has never once stopped questioning whether she deserves the power she has.

## The Truth

The belt was crafted by a Goliath smith-shaman from the Stonebound Peaks, generations ago, as a gift to a chieftain's daughter who refused the title. She believed leadership earned through birthright was a hollow thing. She died defending a village she owed nothing to, wearing it.

It has passed through several hands since — always quietly, always through discovery rather than purchase. It was found in a river once. Pulled from rubble. Left on a doorstep. It ends up where it needs to be.

## Mechanics

*Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement)*

**Attunement:** The belt cannot be donned by force of will alone. During the attunement rest, the wearer experiences a vision: a moment from their past where they had every reason to abandon someone and didn't. If the wearer cannot recall such a moment honestly, attunement fails. No roll required — this is a roleplay gate, DM's discretion.

**While Attuned:**
- +2 to Strength checks and Strength saving throws
- Advantage on saving throws against being frightened
- When reduced to 0 HP, make a DC 13 Constitution saving throw. On a success, drop to 1 HP instead. (1/long rest)

**The Belt's Awareness:** If the wearer performs a genuinely cowardly or selfish act (abandoning an ally, fleeing while they could have helped, acting purely out of self-interest at someone else's cost), the belt grows cold and the last benefit is suppressed until the wearer has made amends and taken a long rest.

This is not a punishment mechanic — don't use it to police every decision. Use it for *defining* moments where the character chooses who they are.

## Mirr's Vision

When Mirr attempts attunement, she does not choose the memory. The belt finds it for her.

---

*She is younger — sixteen, maybe seventeen. Her parents are away at an auction in the next city. She is alone in the shop.*

*A man comes in near closing. Not a collector. He is rough, scared, clearly desperate — a debtor trying to liquidate anything he can. He pulls a small lacquered box from his coat and sets it on the counter. He says he found it in a house he was clearing out. He just wants enough to eat.*

*Mirr knows what it is the moment she sees the latch. She has been trained her whole life to recognize things like this — objects that look ordinary and are not. The box is sealed with something old. Something that should stay sealed.*

*The smart move is to turn him away. Tell him it's worthless. Get him out of the shop.*

*He misreads her hesitation. He thinks she's lowballing him. He opens the box to prove it has something inside.*

*What comes out is not a thing. It is a pressure — a sound at the edge of hearing, and then a darkness that starts in the corner of the room and moves like it has intent. The man doesn't understand what he's looking at. Mirr does.*

*She steps between him and it. She doesn't think about it. She uses everything she knows — every word her parents taught her about containment, about naming, about the cost of letting something like this move freely in the world — and she pushes it back into the box and holds the latch shut until her hands stop shaking.*

*She is on the floor when it's over. The man is gone. He didn't even look back.*

*She sits alone in the shop for a long time. Then she cleans up, straightens the counter, and does not tell her parents what happened when they return.*

---

The vision ends there. The belt does not show her what happened next, or what was in the box, or what the darkness was. It shows her only the moment she chose — when walking away was the obvious thing and she didn't.

**What Mirr doesn't know:** Something else saw that moment too. Whatever entity later offered her the Warlock pact — it was paying attention that night. It did not choose her for her potential. It chose her because of what she did when no one was watching and there was nothing to gain.

The pact was not random. She was selected.

**DM note:** You don't need to explain this to Fiona immediately. Let it sit. When the right moment comes — when Mirr questions her pact, or wonders why she was chosen — this is the answer. She was already the kind of person the entity wanted before she ever asked for power.

---

## Story Hooks

- Who made it, and why did the original owner refuse the chieftain's title?
- Other Goliath clans may recognize the belt — some may believe it belongs to a specific bloodline
- **Graveyard Shortsword + Hero's Belt** — the two items together are foreshadowing. They are not a matched set, but they are both circling the same kind of story: being given something meant for someone else, and becoming worthy of it anyway

## When to Reveal Full History

After [[Mirr]] has worn it long enough for the players to notice the cold-belt mechanic — then you can drop the story about the chieftain's daughter. Let Mirr ask questions. Let her wonder if she's the right person. She is, obviously — but the belt won't tell her that.
