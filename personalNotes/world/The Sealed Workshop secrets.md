# The Sealed Workshop — DM Secrets

*Public file: [[The Sealed Workshop]]*

## Why It Was Sealed

Thirty years ago, the workshop's owners — a Thelasian craft family operating out of [[Aussen]] — discovered that the kiln they had been using for generations was built directly above a root network junction: a point where multiple deep root systems converge underground. They had been unknowingly using heat from the junction to fire their ceramics. The pieces were subtly infused with root energy as a result.

The infusion was inert in most pieces. In a handful, it was not.

Three collectors who purchased specific pieces reported the same dream over consecutive nights — roots, warmth, darkness — and one reported that a ceramic bowl had cracked open on its own, sprouting a small flowering plant overnight.

The family contacted the regional authority and requested a quiet closure. They did not want the building destroyed — the kilns are irreplaceable — but they could not continue producing goods they didn't fully understand.

The authority sealed it. The record was made administrative to avoid panic. The family moved away from [[Aussen]] within a year.

## The Back Entrance

There is a second entrance — a low tunnel cut into the basalt at the back of the building, originally used to haul clay from the riverbed. It was sealed with a wooden hatch, not the administrative ironwood and chain.

The hatch has been forced recently. Someone has been inside.

## What's Inside

- The kilns (three of them, large, Thelasian-cut stone)
- Shelves of unfinished pieces — ceramics at various stages of production, still waiting to be fired
- A workshop ledger listing every piece ever sold and its buyer — including [[Mirr]]'s parents as a regular buyer in the years before closure
- A lower storage room with fired pieces that were never shipped — the ones the family considered unstable

The unstable pieces are still there. They are not dangerous unless broken. If broken, they release concentrated root energy — the equivalent of a minor blood root exposure in a small area.

## Mirr's Parents

The ledger lists [[Mirr]]'s parents as buyers across a fifteen-year period. They purchased regularly and in volume. They are named.

This means one of two things — DM chooses:

**Option A:** Her parents knew about the root infusion and sourced these pieces deliberately. They have been selling subtly enchanted goods for years without disclosing this to buyers. The family business is built partly on this.

**Option B:** Her parents had no idea. They bought good Thelasian craft and sold good Thelasian craft. When they hear the workshop's mark is appearing on fresh goods, they will be alarmed — and they will want to know who has been inside.

## Who Has Been Inside

Someone with knowledge of the back tunnel has been removing pieces from the unshipped stock and selling them through frightened intermediaries. The goods reaching [[Thornhaven]]'s market are from the sealed shelves, not fresh production.

The seller is not named yet — DM to decide. Candidates:
- A descendant of the original family who returned to [[Aussen]]
- An agent of [[Velthara]] who traced the root-infused pieces through old buyer records
- [[Dr Clair Bucklebub]], who found the tunnel while surveying near the Thelasian ruins

## Connection to the Ruins

The root network junction beneath the workshop is the same network that runs through the [[Rootmother's Garden]] and beneath the Thelasian ruins near [[Aussen]]. The junction is a node — a point where the roots are dense enough that the ground above stays slightly warmer year-round.

The Thelasian civilization knew about these nodes. The ruins near [[Aussen]] were built on one deliberately.
