## The Familiar Face — Thalen's Disappearance

### What Aeris Knows

Her father, Thalen Rook — a human playwright — vanished during a private performance for Elarion's high court when she was twelve. He left behind a page torn from a script, stained in blood and ink, and his silver-stringed lyre, which he had commissioned in secret for her. Engraved beneath the frame: *"Let her voice carry what mine could not."*

Her mother, Lirae Whisperwind, stayed silent about what happened. Aeris never forgave that silence. She left Elarion and has been walking the world since, listening for anything that might lead her to the truth.

**After S07 — added when she sees the face at [[Cross Bones Pub]]:**
- During her evening set, she recognised a face in the crowd from Elarion's court. Not a name. A face — one that was there the night Thalen performed his last show.
- When she looked directly at them, they left. She followed; the street was empty.
- She doesn't know who they are. She knows they recognised her too — they didn't look away immediately before leaving.

### DM Truth

**Who the face belongs to:**
[[Sera Voss]] — a former low-ranking court attendant from Elarion. She arranged logistics for private performances at the high court, including Thalen's last one.

She is now in Thornhaven. She watched the full performance before leaving. She is not running — she is deciding whether to approach.

**What Sera Voss knows:**
DM chooses one of the options in [[Sera Voss]]:
- She witnessed what happened to Thalen and has been carrying it alone since
- She was sent from Elarion to find Aeris, and is having second thoughts
- She knew Thalen personally and is in Thornhaven for her own reasons

**Her next move:**
She will find a way to approach Aeris privately — when the rest of the party is not present. Her opening will be something only someone at that court performance would know: a detail, a line from the script, something specific that proves she was there.

**The deeper thread:**
Thalen's disappearance was not random. His performance was a private commission for the high court — someone specific commissioned it. Whatever Thalen found in that performance, or whatever he said, or whatever he was asked to do, is the reason he vanished. Sera Voss knows some of this. She does not know all of it.

**What happened to Thalen:**
DM to decide. The lyre responding to Aeris's emotions (Whisperwind Quill now behaving similarly) suggests Thalen had some connection to the kind of magic that responds to intent and feeling — bardic resonance that goes deeper than performance. He may still be alive, held somewhere, unable to speak. The blood and ink on the torn script page suggest violence — but not necessarily death.

## The Whisperwind Quill — Blood Root Thread

### What Aeris Knows

She received the Whisperwind Quill from [[Pipe Ashford]] after the attunement ritual. It hums near deliberate lies. It occasionally writes words she didn't intend at the margins of her notes.

Small plants and flowers sometimes push through cracks near her when she plays. She has started to notice.

### DM Truth

See [[09-blood-root-effects]] — Aeris's section. The quill is amplifying what the blood root is doing to her. The marginal writing it produces is not random — it is true, and increasingly it will say things she is actively suppressing. Her music is becoming a channel for something she cannot fully control.

This will be most visible in high-emotion moments: confronting her mother's silence, hearing Thalen's name, playing for someone who is lying to her.
