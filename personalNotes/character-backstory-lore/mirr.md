## The Sealed Workshop — Her Parents' Trade

### What Mirr Knows

Her parents deal in rare and powerful artifacts — she grew up learning to appraise, negotiate, and handle objects that could be as dangerous as they were valuable. She left to source for herself: resale, study, personal power.

**After S07 — added when she spots the market stall:**
- She found goods bearing [[The Sealed Workshop]]'s maker's mark in [[Thornhaven]]'s morning market. The pieces were fresh — clean clay in the grooves.
- Her parents sourced from this workshop regularly. It was sealed by regional authority approximately thirty years ago.
- The stall owner bought the pieces from a frightened man passing through from the direction of [[Aussen]].
- Someone is inside the sealed building removing stock.

**If she investigates at the [[Library]] (History DC 14 with [[Anni]]):**
- The closure record is administrative, no stated reason.
- The workshop was deliberately and quietly sealed — not destroyed.
- She does not yet know her parents appear in the workshop's ledger.

### DM Truth

**Why the workshop was sealed:**
The kilns were built above a root network junction — a convergence point in the deep root system. The firing process was unknowingly infusing pieces with root energy. Most pieces: inert. Some: not. When three collectors reported identical dreams and one piece cracked open and sprouted a plant overnight, the workshop family contacted the authority and quietly requested closure. See [[The Sealed Workshop secrets]].

**Mirr's parents in the ledger:**
The workshop's internal ledger — still inside the building — lists Mirr's parents as regular buyers across fifteen years of operation. They purchased in volume.

DM chooses one:
- **They knew.** Her parents were aware the pieces were subtly root-infused and sourced them deliberately. The family business includes items with properties that were never disclosed to buyers. Mirr has been unknowingly selling enchanted goods her whole life.
- **They didn't know.** They bought good Thelasian craft and sold it as such. When they learn the workshop's mark is appearing on fresh goods, they will be alarmed — and very motivated to know who has the back entrance.

**Who is inside the workshop:**
Someone with knowledge of the back tunnel (a low wooden hatch at the rear, used originally for clay hauling) has been removing pieces from the unshipped stock. The pieces reaching the market are from shelves that have been sitting sealed for thirty years — not fresh production.

The seller is frightened and working through intermediaries. DM to name them. Candidates in [[The Sealed Workshop secrets]].

**The connection to the ruins:**
The root network junction beneath [[The Sealed Workshop]] is the same network running beneath [[Rootmother's Garden]] and the Thelasian ruins near [[Aussen]]. The ruins were built on a node deliberately. [[Velthara]] is aware of the network. She is interested in the workshop's location for the same reason she is interested in the garden.

**What finding the workshop could mean for Mirr:**
If she finds the ledger and sees her parents' names — this is the moment her family's business becomes complicated. The question of whether they knew changes everything about who she thinks they are.

## The Pactbreaker Shard — Blood Root Thread

### What Mirr Knows

She received the Pactbreaker Shard from [[Pipe Ashford]] after the attunement ritual. It goes cold sometimes for no visible reason. After using warlock magic, she's noticed root-like burn marks in the residue.

### DM Truth

See [[09-blood-root-effects]] — Mirr's section. The blood root is in tension with her warlock pact — her patron has noticed the attunement and is not pleased. The cold moments from the Shard are her patron probing the new connection. The root-like burn marks are the blood root asserting itself over the pact's usual signature.

This will eventually force a choice: the root network and the patron want different things from Mirr, and she is the point where they meet.
