# Age of Roots — Deep Lore Plan

> DM Planning Document. Tracks what's being written, why, and how it connects.

---

## Objective

Create rich, interconnected pre-history lore that gives the campaign meaningful weight and drives toward an epic endgame. Every current mystery should have an ancient root. Every faction should trace back to a single origin event.

---

## Core Narrative Spine

### The Greedy God
**Gorethis the Blooming** (later: the Verdant Tyrant) — god of growth and cultivation — discovered that Blood root, cultivated with divine intent, amplifies and returns power tenfold. He called this the **Verdant Cycle** and spent 50 years building the largest Blood root garden in history, aiming to absorb enough divine essence to surpass all other gods.

### The Two Gods Who Stopped Him
- **Scarsith** (god of scarcity/survival) detected the "false fullness" in the divine realm and identified the Verdant Cycle's mechanism. His solution: scarcity, not destruction — keep Blood root fragmented across many desperate users so it never accumulates.
- **Maerath** (god of fate/memory/portals) created **Velthara** as a living counter-weapon — a predator built from threads of divine will, designed to consume Blood root and with it Gorethis's essence.

### What Blood Root Actually Is
Blood root is Gorethis. His divine essence is dispersed into every plant. The amplification property is not botanical — it is Gorethis still responding to intent, still cycling. He is dormant, not dead.

### The Rootmother
**Maeris Thornweave** — Gorethis's head druid, who believed she was healing the land. When the truth was revealed, she absorbed the last surge of the Verdant Cycle into her own body (breaking the cycle), died doing it, and bound her life force to the garden as the Root Guardian. She has been watching, and warning, for 600 years. Pipe Ashford is her current student — Pipe just doesn't know it yet.

### The Wizard
**Aeldris Vorn** — Thelasian archmage and maintainer of Maerath's portal network. When called to help, he:
1. Built the water channels that still feed Thornhaven today
2. Raised the Veiled Peaks around the garden (using all portal network energy in one discharge — dooming Thelasian civilization)
3. Wove a memory-suppression thread into Velthara

He wrote a confession journal, sealed it in the deepest Thelasian ruin chamber. His research tower still exists in the Veiled Peaks — it contains the Verdant Cycle mechanics AND the method for controlled dispersal (the endgame key).

### Scarsith's Long Game
Thorhaven was not a lucky settlement. Scarsith guided displaced survivors from the Jackal War to that exact valley to create a permanent scarcity anchor — many people using Blood root for many mundane purposes, fragmenting divine accumulation so Gorethis can never converge.

### The Irony That Drives Everything
Velthara was built by Maerath to consume Gorethis. She did her job. She is *still* doing her job — consuming Blood root, accumulating Gorethis's essence inside herself. She has become exactly what she was created to destroy. She is the new Verdant Cycle.

**The trap:** Killing Velthara releases all of Gorethis's fragments inside her at once. He converges. He wakes. The players cannot simply kill the villain.

### Scarsith's Position
Not one of the three "central" gods in the event, but the one who identified it, shaped the long-game solution, and is most aware of current events. He is god of survival — he endures, he calculates, he does not intervene directly. He reaches out through his faithful. His Thornhaven church is compromised by Velthara, which is working against his plan without Nell realizing it.

## Plot Twists (Outline — detail in plot-twists.md)

1. **The Owl Curse is a protection** — Maerath transformed the druids to hide them. When Maerath wakes, it can be reversed.
2. **Blood root IS Gorethis** — Every plant is a fragment of a sleeping deity.
3. **Thornhaven was planned** — Scarsith guided the founders here deliberately.
4. **Velthara was created by a god** — She is Maerath's weapon, now broken.
5. **Killing Velthara releases Gorethis** — The players cannot simply fight their way out.
6. **Aeldris doomed his civilization on purpose** — The Thelasian collapse was one man's sacrifice.
7. **The endgame key is in the tower** — Controlled dispersal of Gorethis's fragments makes it safe to stop Velthara.
8. **Eman's green eyes** — Aasimar heritage. Gorethis's color. She may be a divine vessel.

## Campaign Endgame Shape

The campaign is building toward a three-problem convergence:
- **Velthara** accumulating enough divine power to trigger spontaneous convergence (Gorethis wakes through her)
- **Maerath waking** unprepared, not knowing what Velthara has become
- **The Rootmother's Garden seal degrading** — when the Root Guardian falls, Gorethis's last fragments are loose

The resolution requires:
1. Find Aeldris's tower (Veiled Peaks, above snow line)
2. Learn the controlled dispersal method
3. Perform dispersal before or during the confrontation with Velthara
4. Decide what to do about Maerath (wake him? use him? ask him to fix the Owlmark?)
5. Decide what to do about Gorethis — scattered fragments can be dispersed permanently into the earth, or... someone could try to consolidate them into a vessel who could contain a god consciously (Eman?)

## Scarsith's Role Summary

Scarsith is not a villain. He is not a patron. He is a chess player who set up a board 300 years ago and is watching his pieces fall out of position. He will help the players if they seek him — through his faithful, through dreams, through rats with knowing eyes. He cannot act directly. He never has. He endures, and he helps the desperate endure.

His message, when the players are ready: *"Do not cut her thread until his fragments are scattered. Find the tower in the peaks."*
