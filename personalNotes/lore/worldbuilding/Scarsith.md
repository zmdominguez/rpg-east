## 👨‍🎤 Description

A great rat. Always depicted as a great rat — not as a metaphor or a symbol, but as his actual chosen form when he appears in visions or divine contact. Dark grey fur, scarred in places, one ear slightly torn. His eyes see in total darkness and glow faintly red in the light. He is never depicted as a majestic creature. He is depicted as exactly the kind of rat you would find in the walls of a building that should have collapsed years ago.

His priests say: *he looks the way he does so that nothing expects much of him.* This is closer to the truth than they know.

In divine form, when he speaks to mortals, the words arrive more as impulse than sound — the sudden certainty, after months of near-starvation, that there is food two streets to the left. The iron conviction that if you just hold on one more day, something will change. He does not comfort. He redirects.

## 📝 Notes

### Mortal Life — Before Ascension

Scarsith was a halfling, which meant he spent his mortal life being underestimated. He trained as a Rogue — specifically the kind of rogue who notices things rather than the kind who steals them. Inquisitive, methodical, pathologically observant. He survived a city siege by being the only person who noticed the guard rotation had a fourteen-minute gap on the eastern wall. He survived a plague by noticing that everyone who drew from the north well got sick and everyone who drew from the south well didn't. He survived poverty by noticing which merchants were skimming their scales and offering to keep quiet in exchange for a cut.

He was never famous. He was never powerful. He was the halfling in the corner who had already clocked every exit before anyone else sat down.

His ascension is unclear even to historians. There was no dramatic moment — no hurricane, no battle, no grand revelation. At some point the halfling who kept surviving impossible situations because he noticed what others missed became the god that desperate people prayed to when survival felt impossible. The prayers found him because he had always been the sort of person who answered them, one small observation at a time.

### During the Age of Roots — A Minor God

At the time of the War of Thorns, Scarsith was small. Few temples, thin following, no armies of the faithful. His domain — scarcity, survival, the desperate hour — is not one that inspires grand civilizations to name their cities after him. He was the god people prayed to after they had already prayed to someone more impressive and received no answer.

What he had, in place of power, was sensitivity. His entire domain is built on recognizing imbalance — the difference between true scarcity and false abundance. When [[Gorethis]]'s Verdant Cycle began distorting the divine realm, creating warmth and surplus that shouldn't exist, a major god might have noticed a pleasant fullness and moved on. Scarsith felt it like a wrong note in a familiar song. He tracked it. He confirmed it. He brought his evidence to [[Maerath]].

That was the full extent of his contribution to the Age of Roots. He was not invited to the confrontation — he was not powerful enough for his presence to mean anything. He was not part of Maerath's solution. He watched from a distance as greater powers solved the problem, and then he watched the catastrophic aftermath unfold, and he stayed awake through all of it, answering prayers.

### How He Grew

The Age of Roots left suffering in its wake at a scale no single god had caused before:

- **[[Velenth]]'s death** caused a three-month famine storm. The starving prayed for survival. Scarsith answered every prayer he could reach. He grew.
- **The Thelasian civilization's collapse** over two centuries produced waves of displaced, hungry, structureless communities with no patron deity. They prayed to whatever was listening. Scarsith was always listening. He grew.
- **The Jackal War** — decades of grinding attrition — created the largest displaced population in centuries. By its end, Scarsith had more temples than he had accumulated in the previous millennium.

He did not cause the suffering. He would not have chosen it. He is what he is *because* of it — a minor deity who answered prayers that fell through the cracks of a divine order that was busy collapsing — and he knows it, and he does not allow himself to forget.

By Year 0 he was no longer minor. He had power, followers, and a plan four centuries in the making.

### The Thornhaven Project

Scarsith understood the solution to Gorethis before Maerath did — he had identified it the moment he understood the Verdant Cycle's mechanism: *keep Blood root constantly fragmented, harvested by desperate people for mundane purposes, never allowed to accumulate.* Scattered, suffering use depletes the divine charge. Gorethis, spread thin across a thousand plants being pulled up by farmers and healers, cannot converge.

He needed to execute this. For centuries he built toward it.

When the Jackal War reached the right level of displacement, Scarsith moved: through his faithful, he planted identical dreams in scattered, homeless survivors. *A valley east, rich with medicine in the mountains, clean water from old channels, a place to start again.* Seven families responded. They did not know each other. They arrived within weeks of one another and called it Thornhaven.

The Church of Scarsith was the first structure built in Thornhaven — before houses, before walls. This was not coincidence. It was infrastructure. His eyes and ears and hands at ground level, ensuring the scarcity engine ran.

He also ensured the settlement was close enough to the [[Halfheight Forest]] to maintain friction with the [[Owl Cult]] — not enough to destroy Thornhaven, but enough that no single faction could monopolize the hills. Competition means no monopoly. A monopoly on Blood root is the one thing that could restart the Verdant Cycle.

### Present Day (Year 5)

Scarsith's containment operation is failing.

[[Velthara]] is extracting Blood root faster than Thornhaven's mundane use can deplete it. The scarcity engine is being outpaced. And [[Nell]], his primary operative in Thornhaven, has been compromised by Velthara — used against his plan without knowing it.

He is routing his communications through less obvious channels. A rat that follows a player. A dream that leaves one clear image: a tower, snow, a door marked with a web. A Scarsith priest who delivers a message they cannot explain receiving: *"Tell them: the Tyrant is not below. He is inside the Weaver. Do not cut her thread until his fragments are scattered. Find the tower in the peaks."*

He cannot act directly. That is not his nature. He is the god of enduring, not the god of arriving. He works through the desperate and the hungry — the ones who have no choice but to gnaw through the obstacle in front of them and trust that the other side exists.

He is watching the players. He has been for some time.

### Key Details

- His greeting among the faithful: *"The rat endures where the lion falls."* Response: *"May your hunger be satisfied."*
- The phrase appears in [[Aeldris Vorn]]'s journal — Aeldris encountered it during his 20-year exile. He had no idea who Scarsith was or that he would eventually matter.
- Scarsith suspects [[Eman]]'s green eyes are Gorethis's color — that she may be an unwitting divine vessel. He has not acted on this suspicion. He is calculating.
- The dark irony he carries: The catastrophes that fed his growth are the same events that require him to act now. He is cleaning up a mess he benefited from. He does not pretend otherwise.
