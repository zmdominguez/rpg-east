## 👨‍🎤 Description

An androgynous figure of middling height, skin pale as moonlight with no warmth in it. Their eyes have no irises — just shifting silver, like cold water catching light, impossible to hold a gaze with because the pupils drift. They move slightly out of phase with time: blink and they are already where you expected them to be in three steps. Their voice arrives with a faint echo, as if spoken half a second before their mouth opens.

Their robes are woven from actual silver thread that catches light at odd angles, never quite matching the illumination in the room. At their fingers, on quiet occasions, thin filaments of glowing thread trail behind every gesture — the invisible strings of fate made momentarily visible.

Their symbol is a spider web with a single golden thread at its center. Every Thelasian ruin, every old shrine, every forgotten artifact from their era bears this mark. Players who see it and later encounter [[Velthara]] — and think carefully — will understand.

## 📝 Notes

### Mortal Life — Before Ascension

Maerath was a High Elf — which meant he had centuries to become what he became. He trained as a Divination Wizard in an elvish academy that no longer exists, driven by a single obsession: the invisible connections between events. Not prophecy exactly. The *structure* of fate — why certain events cluster, why certain people meet, why consequences ripple the way they do.

He spent three centuries mapping these threads before he discovered he could touch them. A century more before he could pull them. By the time he was considered a deity rather than an extraordinarily powerful wizard, the distinction was philosophical. His portals began as advanced Conjuration theory — if fate connects two points, a sufficiently precise spell can make that connection physical and traversable. The portal network the Thelasians used began as his doctoral research, five hundred years before the Thelasian civilization existed.

He ascended not through worship but through mastery — he understood the weave of fate so completely that he became part of it. He was the first deity in this world's history to be more scholar than symbol.

- God of fate, memory, connections, and portals. Controlled the physical portal network used by the [[Thelasian Ruins|Thelasian civilization]].
- Closest divine ally of [[Scarsith]]. Where Scarsith identifies problems through instinct and the feeling of imbalance in his bones, Maerath maps it — traces every thread, models every outcome.
- When Scarsith brought evidence of [[Gorethis]]'s Verdant Cycle, Maerath spent ten years running projections. His conclusion: the only solution was a predator built to consume Blood Root faster than Gorethis could cycle it.
- **Creator of [[Velthara]].** Wove her over five years from threads of pure divine will — not a transformed animal, not a mortal soul, but something genuinely new. He gave her hunger, intelligence, and a body that could consume divine essence without being destroyed by it. He did not give her memory of her origin. He told himself this was temporary — that he would restore her memories once the crisis passed.
- He fell asleep before he could.
- Called on [[Aeldris Vorn]] to complete the work he was too exhausted to finish: raise the mountains, seal the portals, weave the memory thread.
- **Current status:** Sleeping beneath the deepest chamber of the Thelasian ruins. He has been sleeping for approximately 600 years. He intended to wake within decades.
- Dr. Clair Bucklebub's excavation in Year 4 cracked the ceiling of his resting place. He is stirring. The portals reactivating are the consequence of his divine presence becoming less dormant.
- Velthara, prowling above him with crimson webs through the ruins, is circling her maker without knowing it.
- **When he wakes:** He will not know the year. He will not know the Thelasian civilization is gone. He will not know what Velthara has become. He can reverse the [[Owl Cult|Owlmark]] — permanently breaking the centuries-old transformation. He can restore Velthara's memories if she accepts it. He is not a solution. He is a god processing 600 years of consequences. He may be a resource, an ally, or another crisis depending on who meets him first.
- Personality: Meticulous, sorrowful, deeply analytical. Does not apologize easily — when he does, it is absolute and complete. Speaks in carefully weighed sentences. Makes terrible choices without flinching and regrets them without forgetting.
