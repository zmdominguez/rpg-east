## 👨‍🎤 Description

Short and stocky with the build of someone who has worked outdoors their entire life — shoulders broad, hands large, posture permanently inclined slightly forward, as if leaning into weather. Hair once brown, fully white by the time she died, worn loose and frequently tangled with small leaves she didn't notice. Eyes the color of turned earth: deep brown with flecks of gold that caught light when she was focused on something.

She moved with deliberate care, as if the ground itself deserved consideration before each step. Spoke rarely in groups, clearly when she did. Had a habit of pressing her palm flat to the earth when thinking — a diagnostic gesture that became unconscious over three decades of practice.

A small, round scar on her left palm from the moment she pressed herself to the garden's center stone to absorb the final surge of the Verdant Cycle. It was the only wound she ever took.

## 📝 Notes

- Born to a farming family. Discovered her druidic abilities at 14. Trained in the old circle traditions, joined [[Gorethis]]'s Circle of the Verdant Root at 28, became head druid at 35.
- Served as head druid of the Circle for 30 years, genuinely believing she was healing the land. [[Gorethis]] told her the Verdant Cycle was "returning divine blessing to the soil." He was not entirely lying. He omitted the destination.
- **Her druidic abilities in practice:**
  - Wildshape: Favored ground animals exclusively. Badger, mole, hedgehog. She had no interest in the sky — the earth was her medium.
  - Signature spells: *Entangle*, *Plant Growth*, *Speak with Plants* (cast without components by the end of her life — she simply knew the language), *Commune with Nature*
  - Unique personal ability — *Verdant Sense*: could feel the divine charge accumulated in Blood Root through touch, like feeling a pulse. Not teachable. Developed over years of Gorethis's cultivation work. This ability is what allowed her to detect the final surge and absorb it deliberately.
- When [[Scarsith]] and [[Maerath]] revealed the truth, she did not speak for three days. Then she went back to the garden.
- She gathered the twelve druids of the Circle and told them what they had been building. Six left. She did not stop them. Six stayed.
- She walked to the center stone without making a speech. The six who stayed understood without being told.
- She pulled the full accumulated divine essence through her own body — used herself as the circuit breaker of the Verdant Cycle. The power destroyed her. She had minutes.
- She spent those minutes binding her life force to the roots.
- **Current state:** Her consciousness is fragmented and slow, stretched across the root system of the [[Rootmother's Garden]] over an area roughly the size of a city block. She experiences time differently — decades feel like days. She cannot speak. She cannot act. She can only influence the behavior of the garden: which direction vines grow, which plants bloom, where the [[Root Guardian]] positions itself.
- She has been trying to communicate with [[Pipe Ashford]] for three years. Pipe has been interpreting the garden's behavior as "the Rootmother's wishes." She is correct. She does not yet understand the urgency of the message.
- **What Maeris needs Pipe to know:** Gorethis is not fully sealed. The fragments inside the garden are weakening as the Root Guardian degrades. When the Guardian falls, the seal breaks. Gorethis's dormant fragments — held in place by Maeris's dying will for six centuries — will be free to respond to the next focused cultivator.
- **She is ending.** Not immortal — a mortal life force stretched catastrophically thin for too long. She is not afraid. She is urgent.
- What she did not know: When she died, she believed she had sealed all of Gorethis. She did not know that [[Velthara]] had already consumed most of his essence and that the largest concentration was already inside the spider, not the garden. She has spent 600 years sealing a fragment, believing she sealed the whole.
- **The detail that illuminates her:** She never named herself the Rootmother. The Owl Cult's descendants gave her that name generations later. She would have found it embarrassing.
