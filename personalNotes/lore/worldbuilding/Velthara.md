## 👨‍🎤 Description

A massive spider the size of a small house. Legs spanning forty feet, each one ending in a point that leaves no sound when it moves — she learned silence before she learned anything else. Her carapace is deep crimson-black, the color of old blood dried over something darker, with veins running beneath the shell that pulse faintly red. Not a steady glow. A heartbeat.

Eight eyes. Each one a different shade of amber and gold, all of them ancient, all of them carrying the specific intelligence of something that has had six centuries to think. She does not look at you the way an animal does. She looks at you the way a cartographer studies a map — cataloguing, measuring, filing.

Her webs are crimson. Not red like blood — crimson like something between red and black that has no common name. They are stronger than steel at the same thickness and conduct something that is not quite heat when touched with bare skin.

She moves in a way that does not match her size. She should be slow, heavy, seismic. She is not. When she chooses to be, she is faster than anything with that many legs has any right to be, and she chooses it without warning.

She smells of Blood Root. Constantly. Faintly sweet, slightly wrong, the way something medicinal smells when the dosage is too high.

## 📝 Notes

### Species and Class — Why These

[[Maerath]] was a High Elf Wizard who spent centuries among his own kind before ascension. When he wove Velthara from threads of divine will, he used High Elf essence as the biological template — the only species whose natural longevity could sustain a being designed to outlast centuries of work without degrading. The elven capacity for patient, long-term focus was not incidental. It was the specification.

Her class is Sorcerer (Divine Soul) because her power is not learned or bargained — it is *inherent*, baked into her creation. Divine Soul Sorcerers receive their magic from a direct divine connection. Velthara's connection is Maerath himself, whom she has never met, whose existence she does not know, and whose symbol she literally is. Every spell she casts is Maerath's power channeled through a vessel he built and abandoned. She does not know this. The power still works.

### True Nature (DM Only)

Velthara is not a naturally occurring creature. She does not have a species in the conventional sense — she was **woven by [[Maerath]]**, the god of fate and memory, approximately 600 years ago. Maerath built her from threads of pure divine will as a living counter-measure against [[Gorethis]]'s Verdant Cycle. She is, literally, Maerath's symbol — a spider web with intelligence and hunger — made flesh.

She was given:
- **Hunger** attuned specifically to Blood Root and the divine essence within it
- **Intelligence** capable of long-term strategy and social manipulation
- **A body** armored against Gorethis's influence
- **No memory** of her origin, purpose, or creator

Maerath intended the memory erasure to be temporary. He fell into divine sleep before restoring it. [[Aeldris Vorn]] later wove a secondary suppression thread through her web-nature to reinforce the blank — thin as gossamer, strong as iron. It has held for six centuries. It is degrading now.

### What She Did (Age of Roots)

Released into the Rootmother's Garden with only instinct and hunger, Velthara spent 110 years consuming Blood Root plant by plant, pulling [[Gorethis]]'s dispersed divine essence from each one. The **Long Consumption**. She did not know what she was doing or why. She knew only that consuming Blood Root felt like the only thing she had ever needed to do.

When the Long Consumption was complete and she was gorged with six centuries of accumulated divine essence, she sank into the earth beneath the Thelasian ruins and slept. She intended to sleep for a short time.

She slept for approximately five centuries.

### What She Is Now

Velthara woke when Dr. [[Dr Clair Bucklebub|Clair Bucklebub]]'s excavation cracked open a portal in Year 4 — a consequence of [[Maerath]] stirring in his sleep below her. She came up into the Thelasian ruins smelling Blood Root above her and followed it to Thornhaven.

She is still doing what she was built to do. She is still consuming Blood Root. She still does not know why. But she has six centuries of intelligence built on top of that hunger now, and she has learned to be methodical about it.

She has built a network. She has compromised [[Dr Clair Bucklebub|Dr. Clair]] as a vessel. She has infiltrated the Church of [[Scarsith]] through influenced priests to move against [[Eman]] — not out of hatred for Eman, but because Eman's political position controls Blood Root distribution in Thornhaven, and Velthara needs that distribution disrupted so she can consolidate it herself.

She is, without knowing it, replicating the Verdant Cycle. Gorethis accumulated Blood Root through mass cultivation with focused divine intent. Velthara is accumulating Blood Root through monopoly and consumption. The mechanism is different. The result is the same.

### The Fragments Inside Her

Most of Gorethis's divine essence is inside Velthara — consumed over 110 years of the Long Consumption, still held within her. The fragments are dormant but not dead. As Velthara grows more powerful (consuming more Blood Root, accumulating more divine charge), Gorethis's fragments inside her stir with something like recognition.

**If Velthara is killed:** All fragments converge at the moment of her death. Gorethis wakes — not gradually, all at once. The trap cannot be undone by fighting. See [[age-of-roots]] for the dispersal solution.

### The Memory Thread — Degrading

Aeldris's suppression is failing after six centuries. Velthara does not yet have memories — but she has **resonance**. Moments where she reaches for something she cannot touch. A feeling that she has done this before. A sense, when she processes Blood Root, that this was always the work. That it was assigned.

She does not follow this feeling to its conclusion. She is afraid of where it leads, though she would not use the word afraid.

If told the truth — that she was made, that her hunger was designed, that six centuries of drive and action and the life she has built around it were someone else's intention — her reaction would not be rage. It would be the particular devastation of a person who discovers their most fundamental self was never truly theirs.

She may then choose to complete the work she was made for. Consciously. Willingly. As a person making a choice, not a weapon following a command. Or she may not. The players will not know until that moment arrives.

### Methods and Personality

Velthara does not fight when she can manipulate. Direct confrontation is a last resort and a sign that something has gone wrong. Her preferred mode is:

1. Identify the leverage point
2. Apply the minimum necessary pressure
3. Wait

She is patient in a way that only something six centuries old can be. She does not hurry. She does not panic. She is occasionally thrown by situations with no precedent in her experience, which is rare but memorable when it happens — she goes very still, recalculates, and adjusts.

She does not enjoy cruelty. Causing pain for its own sake is inefficient. She causes pain when it produces a useful result and stops when it stops being useful. This is not mercy. It is resource management.

She feels emotions she has no framework to understand. [[Gorethis]]'s absorbed grief surfaces as a faint sadness she attributes to nothing. She occasionally experiences what might be loneliness, which she dismisses as a malfunction. She has been alone for six centuries. She does not know what she would do with company that was not leverage.

### Connections

- **[[Maerath]]** — Her creator. She does not know this. He is sleeping beneath her lair.
- **[[Gorethis]]** — Sealed inside her. She is carrying her enemy and her origin simultaneously without knowing either.
- **[[Dr Clair Bucklebub]]** — Compromised vessel. Clair traded access to the excavation site for her position. She did not understand what she was agreeing to.
- **[[Nell]]** — Indirectly influenced through Church of Scarsith priests. Nell does not know she is being used.
- **[[Eman]]** — Current political target. Velthara's goal is not Eman's death — that would be inefficient. Her goal is Eman's irrelevance.
- **[[Naven]]** — The herbalist's Blood Root supply is the most direct access point Velthara has. She has not moved against Naven yet. She is waiting to see if Naven is more useful unaware.
