## 👨‍🎤 Description

Tall and angular with the lean build of someone who spent decades in narrow underground passages — elbows and collarbones sharp, posture slightly stooped from ducking through low-ceilinged corridors. Thelasian coloring: skin pale enough to show the faint blue-white luminescence typical of the underground-adapted subrace, visible as a subtle glow in the veins of his hands and neck in total darkness. Hair kept white-silver and tied back with a leather cord he replaced so often the knot was different in every portrait. Eyes the color of deep cave water — very dark, very still, the quality of something that has never been rushed.

He wore practical robes with an unreasonable number of pockets, each labeled in his own notation system that no one else ever fully decoded. In his final twenty years of exile: weathered, lean as driftwood, dressed in improvised layers. He still kept his notes. He annotated them during the journey south. Several pages of the journal found in the Thelasian ruins are written on the backs of bark he stripped from trees, the ink made from Blood Root.

## 📝 Notes

- Born in the Thelasian deep-city of Vethras, the largest of the underground settlements. Rose through the arcane academies to become the civilization's foremost authority on ley line manipulation and dimensional anchoring.
- Title of "Thread-Keeper" was professional, not devotional — the Thelasians maintained [[Maerath]]'s portal network the way a harbor master maintains a seaport. Aeldris was the harbor master.
- **Specialization — Portal Architecture:** Trained in ley line redirection, dimensional anchoring, portal construction and maintenance, spatial geometry. The raising of the [[Veiled Peaks]] was not a spell. It was an engineering act: he identified five ley line convergences beneath the highland valley, calculated the force distribution required to achieve vertical displacement without collapse, and discharged all accumulated portal network energy through a single point simultaneously. The mountains grew over three days. He was deaf in his left ear permanently after.
- **Other notable spells:** *Teleport* (native), *Gate* (required the network infrastructure), *Demiplane*, *Sequester* (used to seal the journal), and uniquely: **Vorn's Thread** — a personal working that permanently linked two spatial points with a traversable connection. Inferior to a gate but required no ongoing maintenance. Several of his Threads still exist in the Veiled Peaks and may be discoverable.
- **Physical limitation:** Could not cast healing magic. Had no interest in it. Would consume raw Blood Root when injured and complain bitterly about the taste. "It tastes like disappointment and old wood," per the journal.
- When [[Maerath]] asked for his help, Aeldris calculated the cost before Maerath finished explaining. Sealing the portal network would isolate the Thelasian civilization. Without trade routes, they would decline. Within 200 years, they would collapse. He agreed immediately.
- His recorded reasoning, from the journal: *"I have lived sixty years in a civilization built on the shame of six druids who chose themselves over the world. I will not be the seventh."*
- **What he did:**
  1. Redirected underground ley lines to create permanent water channels flowing south from the highland valley — still functioning, still feeding [[Thornhaven]]'s irrigation. Designed to last 1,000 years without maintenance. Current steward: [[Venn]].
  2. Raised the [[Veiled Peaks]] around the [[Rootmother's Garden]] using the full discharge of the portal network.
  3. Sealed the portal network to protect Thelasian civilians from [[Gorethis]]'s remaining followers.
  4. Wove Aeldris's Forgetting Thread into [[Velthara]]'s sleeping mind — a suppression woven through her web-nature, reinforcing the gap where her origin should be.
  5. Wrote the journal. Sealed it in the deepest Thelasian chamber in a ley-warded stone box that required a portal-key to open.
- **After the sealing:** Could not portal home. Walked south through the mountains for twenty years. Died somewhere north of what would eventually become [[Thornhaven]]. His body was never found.
- **His tower:** Built into the northeast face of what became the Veiled Peaks, 25 years before the raising, as a maintenance station for the water channel ley lines. Stone construction, four levels, exterior door marked with Maerath's symbol. Interior contents:
  - Full Verdant Cycle mechanics and research notes
  - The controlled dispersal method — the only safe way to scatter Gorethis's fragments from Velthara before she is stopped
  - Three failed attempts to reverse-engineer the Forgetting Thread (he felt responsible for Velthara's blank mind and tried, repeatedly, to undo it — he never succeeded)
  - An incomplete automaton golem he was building during quieter years. It is still there, sitting in a chair, unfinished. The runes for locomotion are carved but not connected.
- **The detail that defines him:** He ate Blood Root every day for twenty years during exile because it was hardy, grew in cold places, and he had little else. Every plant he consumed contained a fragment of [[Gorethis]]. Gorethis, dormant and scattered across a thousand plants, experienced the sensation of being eaten by the man who helped cage him. For twenty years. Aeldris did not know this. [[Scarsith]] did, eventually, and found it to be among the more satisfying ironies of his long career.
- Personality: Precise, duty-bound, and quietly sardonic when not under pressure. Kept records of everything. Annotated his own annotations. Deeply uncomfortable with grand gestures and terrible at goodbyes — the journal's final lines trail off mid-sentence, not from weakness but because he simply ran out of bark.
