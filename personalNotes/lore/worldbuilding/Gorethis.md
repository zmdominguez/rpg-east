## 👨‍🎤 Description

A tall humanoid figure with skin the deep brown of rich, turned soil. His hair blooms constantly — flowers open and close with his mood, white blossoms in calm, red-gold in anger, pale grey in grief. Eyes like sunflowers: golden irises with wide dark centers that seem to absorb light. 

His robes are living vines, perpetually growing, never touching the ground. He has a warm, unhurried smile — the smile of someone with infinite patience — that never quite reaches the eyes. His hands leave faint impressions wherever they rest: pressed briefly on a table, a ring of moss grows in the shape of his palm.

He was, by all accounts, genuinely beautiful. This is important. His corruption was not monstrous. It arrived like a vine grows — slowly, quietly, until the wall is already covered.

## 📝 Notes

### Mortal Life — Before Ascension

Gorethis was born human, the third son of a farming family in a region that suffered drought more years than not. He showed druidic aptitude young — plants grew faster where he walked, livestock calmed at his voice. By his thirties he was the most accomplished druid of his generation, practicing Circle of the Land magic tied to grassland and cultivated farmland. His rituals healed blighted crops. His communities ate when neighboring ones starved. He was genuinely beloved.

His ascension was gradual, not a single moment. Decades of devoted worship, decades of answered prayers, a slow accumulation of divine resonance until the boundary between "very powerful druid" and "minor deity" ceased to be meaningful. He never stopped thinking like a farmer: patient, long-horizon, focused on yield. This is what made the Verdant Cycle feel natural to him. He had been tending growth and harvest his entire mortal life. He simply stopped seeing a reason to limit the scale.

- God of growth, agricultural abundance, and the turning of seasons. Once among the most beloved of all deities.
- Discovered the **Verdant Cycle**: fragments of divine essence fed into [[Blood Root]] during cultivation are amplified and returned at harvest. Spent 50 years refining the process before revealing it.
- Founded the **Circle of the Verdant Root** — a druidic circle tasked (unknowingly) with building his divine battery. Head druid was [[Maeris Thornweave]].
- His goal was never destruction — he wanted to become the *All-God*, a single benevolent shepherd of all creation. He believed, with genuine conviction, that this would be better for everyone. He was catastrophically wrong.
- Detected by [[Scarsith]] who identified the "false fullness" spreading through the divine realm.
- Created [[Velthara]] was woven by [[Maerath]] as a counter-predator — a being designed to consume Blood Root and with it Gorethis's essence. Velthara worked. Over 110 years she devoured most of his divine presence from the garden.
- When [[Maeris Thornweave]] broke the last cycle by absorbing the final surge, Gorethis was reduced to scattered fragments — dormant, not dead.
- **Current status:** His essence is dispersed in every Blood Root plant in the world. The amplification property IS Gorethis, still responding to intent from dormancy. Most of his fragments are now inside [[Velthara]], consumed over six centuries.
- **The trap:** If Velthara is killed, Gorethis's fragments inside her will converge at the moment of her death. He wakes — not gradually, all at once.
- **The detail that haunts:** He wept when Velthara began consuming his garden. He had no body left to cry with, so the sorrow went into the plants. Velthara, made without emotions, has felt inexplicable grief her entire existence. She doesn't know where it comes from. It is Gorethis.
- His worshippers still exist, scattered. What few prayers reach "the Blooming Lord" are answered by something — the fragments, still cycling. The prayers never go unanswered. They are simply answered very, very slowly.
