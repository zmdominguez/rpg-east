## 👨‍🎤 Description

Young-seeming for a god, with the energy of someone who has never once considered caution. Hair perpetually wind-tossed and crackling with visible static. Eyes like lightning — white-blue, quick, never still. He arrived everywhere like weather: ahead of schedule and louder than expected.

Everything about him was dramatic by nature — not performance, just constitution. His laughter caused distant rumbles. When he walked into a room, the candles flickered toward him. He was the sort of god who made great stories because he was constitutionally unable to be boring.

There are no accurate portraits of him. Every artist who attempted one complained that he kept moving.

## 📝 Notes

### Mortal Life — Before Ascension

Velenth was born during a hurricane. His mother survived; the building did not. He manifested Storm Sorcery in adolescence the way some people manifest a temper — suddenly, completely, with no warning and no off switch. A fishing village outside a coastal city. A boy who kept accidentally calling lightning when he laughed too hard. He never trained formally. There was no tradition for what he was.

He traveled. He was useful in storms — he could calm them, or direct them, or become them when he had to. He became a sailor's patron saint before he became a god, in the informal way that works: someone survives something impossible and says *the Storm-Singer protected me* and someone else hears it and says the same thing, and eventually the prayers become real because enough people believe them.

His ascension was the noisiest in recorded history. A hurricane that lasted eleven days. Everyone in a hundred miles knew something had changed. He emerged from it as a deity, grinning, with his hair still standing on end. He never lost that quality — the sense that divinity sat on him loosely, like a coat he hadn't broken in yet.

- Minor deity of storms, sea weather, dramatic atmospheric events, and the chaos of the sky. Beloved by sailors, travelling performers, and anyone whose livelihood depended on reading the sky.
- Not a primary strategic player in the War of Thorns. He showed up to the confrontation with [[Gorethis]] because [[Scarsith]] and [[Maerath]] were going, and he thought it would be an important moment. He was right. It was his last.
- [[Gorethis]], wielding the accumulated Verdant Cycle power, grew Blood Root vines through the storm-channels that carried Velenth's divine presence. The vines smothered the sky. His essence scattered as a three-month thunderstorm that killed crops across the known world.
- **He is the only character in this story who died simply because he wanted to help.**
- His death caused a famine. That famine, centuries of compounding hardship later, contributed to the conditions that produced the Jackal War. The Jackal War produced the displaced survivors [[Scarsith]] guided to found [[Thornhaven]]. Velenth's death is, in a roundabout way, why Thornhaven exists.
- He is not remembered. There are no temples. There is no mythology, no prayer in common circulation, no mention in any surviving text accessible to the players.
- Somewhere in the world there is one remaining temple maintained by one aged priest who does not know his god is dead. The dramatic weather Velenth governed continues without him — storms do not require a god's ongoing supervision. The priest prays into silence every morning. The silence does not feel different from attention.
- Players may find, in very old ruins or carvings, a figure with lightning in his hands. No name attached. No context. Just a symbol that no living person can identify. This is intentional. This is what death looks like for a deity in this world: not a monument, just a mark no one can read.
- **The tragedy to sit with:** Three gods confronted Gorethis. One died for nothing gained. The war continued for another century after his death without him.
