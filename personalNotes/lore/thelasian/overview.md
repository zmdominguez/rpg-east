# Thelasian Civilization — Overview and History

> DM Eyes Only. See [[ruins-guide]] for the current state of the ruins and what players can find there.

---

## WHAT THE THELASIANS WERE

The Thelasian civilization was the most technically sophisticated society in the campaign's recorded history — and one of the least known. They built underground, traded through magical portals, cultivated food in permanent darkness, and constructed golems that still sweep the floors of their ruins five centuries after the last inhabitant died.

They built it all to prove a point.

The civilization was founded by six people who had served a god and watched him try to use their devotion to destroy everything. When they ran, they ran from divinity itself. Their founding law — never codified, always understood — was this: *we do not rely on gods*. Every technological leap the Thelasians made was a direct response to that law. Bioluminescence instead of divine fire. Chemistry instead of healing miracles. Golems instead of divine servants. Portals maintained by arcane engineers instead of granted by prayer.

They succeeded, largely. They built something extraordinary. Then a mortal made one necessary sacrifice, and the infrastructure it destroyed took the civilization with it over the course of two quiet centuries.

The ruins sit beneath a swamp near [[Thornhaven]]. They have been sealed for 350 years. They are not empty.

## FOUNDING — THE SIX WHO FLED (Year -355 to -340)

When [[Maeris Thornweave]] absorbed the final surge of [[Gorethis]]'s Verdant Cycle and bound herself to the Rootmother's Garden, twelve druids of the Circle of the Verdant Root faced a choice. Six stayed. Six ran.

The six who ran were not cowards — or not only cowards. Some calculated survival. Some could not face what they had built. Some did not believe staying would accomplish anything. The specifics varied. The result was the same: six people, carrying only what they knew, moving south and east as fast as guilt would carry them.

They went underground within weeks. Part instinct, part practicality — Gorethis still had divine followers who knew their faces. Surface travel was dangerous. And one among them, the dwarf Thael Ironroot, said a thing that became the civilization's founding logic: *"Underground is just a mine you live in. I know mines."*

They found a cavern system three months into their flight. Deep, dry, structurally sound, fed by an underground river. Thael said it would hold. Brenn Castwater looked at the cave walls, found a species of bioluminescent glowworm already living there, and said: *"We have light."*

They called the first settlement **Vethras** — a word from Thael's dwarven dialect meaning "shelter found."

See [[the-six-founders]] for full profiles of each founding member.

## RISE — THE THELASIAN GOLDEN AGE (Year -340 to -400)

Over the following century, Vethras grew from a survival camp into a city. Then two more cities followed.

The growth was not miraculous — it was systematic. Each of the six founders contributed a different essential capability, and their descendants maintained and expanded those capabilities as distinct castes or traditions:

- **Architecture and construction** (Thael Ironroot's line): underground passage design, load-bearing vault mathematics, drainage engineering
- **Agriculture** (Brenn Castwater's line): bioluminescent glowworm cultivation, fungal farming, the underground water-garden system
- **Medicine** (Senne Ashvale's line): the cleansing pool system, herbal chemistry without divine components, surgical traditions
- **Golem technology** (Wick Tallow's line): increasingly sophisticated animated constructs, the janitor-class, the porter-class, eventually the guardian-class
- **Archives and records** (Morden Far's line): the preservation of history, the Thelasian script, the educational tradition
- **Portal engineering** (Orra Dusk's line, eventually Aeldris Vorn): maintaining the threads of [[Maerath]]'s portal network, later expanding it

The three cities at peak:
- **Vethras** — the capital; portal hubs, the great academy, the administrative core; population ~40,000 at peak
- **Delrath** — built deeper east, mining and metallurgy focus; provided copper and iron for golem construction
- **Ossevane** — built near underground rivers, agricultural focus; Brenn Castwater's descendants maintained the largest cultivated caverns

The Thelasians worshipped Maerath above all others, in a specific and limited way: they credited him for the portal network and asked nothing else of him. No miracles, no intercession, no divine favor. Just the portals, which worked on physical principles they could understand and maintain. This felt acceptable to a civilization that had sworn off divine dependence. They told themselves they were simply grateful for the infrastructure, not the god.

They were more grateful than they admitted. Maerath's presence in the portal threads meant the civilization felt, faintly, watched over. Protected. When [[Aeldris Vorn]] finally sealed the network, the loss was not only logistical.

## THE SEALING AND DECLINE (Year -348 to -150)

In Year -348, Aeldris Vorn — the greatest Thread-Keeper Vethras had ever produced, the man who had maintained Maerath's portal network for thirty years — discharged every unit of accumulated portal energy in a single geological event that raised the [[Veiled Peaks]] around the [[Rootmother's Garden]], sealing the last of Gorethis's essence from the world.

To do this, he destroyed the portal network entirely.

He did not come home. The portals sealed behind him. The trade routes to the surface stopped overnight.

The Thelasians waited for him. For two years they sent messages through the residual network fragments, which barely functioned. For five years they sent scouts to the portal entrance points, which no longer opened. For ten years they debated what had happened. Then they stopped debating and started rationing.

**The timeline of decline:**

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| -348 | Portals sealed. Aeldris gone. Trade routes cut entirely. |
| -340 | First food shortages. Surface imports represented 30% of Vethras's caloric intake. |
| -330 | Golem labor force begins failing — portal energy was the power source. Manual labor returns for first time in generations. |
| -320 | Vethras sends delegations to Delrath and Ossevane for resource sharing. The other cities, facing their own shortages, begin restricting passage. |
| -300 | Delrath seals its main passages to Vethras. Refuses further contact. Resource hoarding begins. |
| -290 | Bioluminescent glowworm cultivation fails in outer sections of Vethras. Population retreats toward the center. |
| -280 | Ossevane loses its underground river system to a geological shift — possibly a delayed consequence of the Veiled Peaks raising. Agricultural output collapses. |
| -250 | Thelasian population at roughly 60% of peak. The archivist caste begins copying records urgently — they know something is ending. |
| -200 | Aeldris's body decomposes somewhere north of where Thornhaven will eventually be built. Nobody underground knows this. Some of the older archivists still believe he will return. |
| -180 | Only Vethras remains meaningfully populated, with a fraction of its peak numbers. The construction tradition builds anyway — the last generation leaves behind the most architecturally precise stonework in the ruins. |
| -150 | Vethras empties. The last inhabitants seal the city from the inside — great stone doors, methodically closed, one per level. They left no note explaining why. They simply finished what they were doing and stopped. |

The inscription found carved above the entrance to the deepest sealed level, dated to this period:
> *"We built because building was the last thing we could do."*

## REDISCOVERY — YEAR 4

The ruins were sealed for approximately 350 years when [[Dr Clair Bucklebub]] led a Cross Bones Inc excavation near the swamp east of Thornhaven. The portal entrance — a stone tablet at the swamp's edge — activated without anyone performing the access ritual.

The reason: [[Maerath]] was stirring in his divine sleep. His presence in the portal network, dormant for six centuries, caused the system to flicker. The portal opened on its own, briefly, before Dr. Clair's team even found it. She documented this as "anomalous geological activity" and filed the correct ritual procedure for future entry.

She did not understand that the portal opening was a symptom of something waking below.

By the time she led the first proper expedition through the cleansing pool and into the town level, [[Velthara]] had been awake for weeks, moving through the deep levels, establishing her territory. Dr. Clair's team reached the town level and found it pristine — the janitor golem still sweeping, the bioluminescent glowworms still active, the cleansing pools still running.

They found the deeper levels two expeditions later. They should have stopped there.

See [[ruins-guide]] for the current state of each level and [[Velthara]] for what she did to Dr. Clair's team.

## WHAT THE THELASIANS DIDN'T KNOW

The founders fled with guilt and knowledge but without complete information. Several things they never learned:

**They didn't know why Aeldris sealed the portals.** They assumed catastrophe, equipment failure, Aeldris's death. They never knew about Gorethis. Morden Far's fragmented records hint at it — he knew about the Verdant Cycle from his time in the Circle — but his descendants didn't understand the context, and the records were copied too many times for the original meaning to survive clearly.

**They didn't know Maerath was sleeping beneath them.** The portal network's ley-line architecture ran down through the rock directly below Vethras. Maerath's divine sleeping place is in the geological layer beneath the city's deepest foundations. The Thelasians built directly above their patron god and never knew he was there.

**They didn't know [[Velthara]] existed.** She had been dormant in the earth before they arrived and stayed dormant throughout the civilization's entire history. The only evidence of her presence in their records is a note from approximately Year -300, in a geological survey of the lower caverns: *"Unusual formations in sub-foundation strata. Not mineral. Possibly biological. Recommend further study."* Nobody followed up.

**They didn't know they were descendants of the people who built the problem.** Morden Far knew. He wrote it down. The copies of his records that survived are too fragmentary for later Thelasians to have understood the full picture. They knew they had founding-ancestors who had "served a god and left." They did not know those ancestors had been cultivating the god's divine battery for thirty years.

This ignorance was, in its way, a mercy.
