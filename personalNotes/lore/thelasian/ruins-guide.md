# Thelasian Ruins — DM Guide (Year 5)

> DM Eyes Only. Level-by-level guide to the ruins as they stand in Year 5. What exists, what's dangerous, what secrets are findable. Cross-reference with [[Velthara]] for her network details and [[age-of-roots]] for the deepest lore.

---

## RUINS OVERVIEW

The ruins occupy six distinct levels beneath the swamp. Each level was sealed from below — great stone doors between levels, methodically closed by the last Thelasians in Year -150. Most of those doors have been reopened by Velthara's servants over the past year.

**Current occupants:**
- **Velthara** — in the deep levels, primarily Level 5. Her lair, her web, her Blood Root farm.
- **Akor** (Drider) — stationed at the boundary between Level 4 and Level 5, acts as Velthara's gatekeeper
- **Bri** (Giant Spider) — patrols Level 3 and above, acts as guide/introducer for visitors Velthara wants to receive
- **Dr. Clair Bucklebub and her 6 researchers** — now vessels, operating on Level 2 and occasionally Level 3 under the pretense of continuing their excavation
- **The janitor golem** — Level 2, unaffiliated, still sweeping
- **Two partially functional porter golems** — Level 4, running old transport routes on residual ley energy, unaffiliated
- **Maerath** — Level 6, sleeping, undetectable

## SURFACE — THE PORTAL ENTRANCE

**Location:** A stone tablet on a raised stone platform at the swamp's edge, approximately 400 meters east of the main road.

**Appearance:** A large flat slab of dark stone engraved with Thelasian script. Appears weathered and old. Does not look like a door — looks like a memorial or a boundary marker.

**Activation:** Visitors must wash their hands in the adjacent stone basin (always filled with groundwater that seeps through from the cleansing pool system below) and press both palms to the tablet surface. The stone becomes briefly warm, the inscription glows pale blue, and a section of the marsh ground sinks to reveal the spiral staircase.

**The inscription** (two lines, large formal Thelasian, readable with DC 14 Intelligence check by a linguist):
> *"Come as you are and be refused*
> *Come as you were and enter true"*

"As you were" means after washing — after cleansing, after honest intent. The portal reads intention at a basic level. It will not open for someone who activates it while actively planning to harm the ruins or their contents. Velthara's servants pass because Velthara wants the ruins intact.

**Current state:** Opens reliably. Since Maerath began stirring, the residual portal energy has stabilized enough for consistent activation. Dr. Clair's team uses it regularly. The players have used it at least once.

## LEVEL 1 — THE THRESHOLD

**Access:** Spiral stone staircase, approximately 40 steps. The walls narrow as you descend — designed to prevent large groups from moving quickly, a subtle defensive measure.

**Bioluminescent glowworms:** First appear on the staircase walls about 10 steps down. By the bottom of the stairs, the tunnel is fully lit in cold blue-white. The light is even, sourceless-seeming, beautiful. Players who succeed on a DC 12 Nature check notice the tiny living organisms responsible — worms no larger than a thumb, embedded in cultivation substrate in the wall niches.

**The tunnel:** A long straight passage extending roughly 60 meters, leading to the cleansing pool chamber. The ceiling is 3 meters high, walls smooth-dressed stone, floor slightly sloped toward the pool to direct water runoff.

**Mushroom hazard:** Two species of fungi grow in the upper sections. One is harmless (edible, slightly bitter). The other releases a spore cloud when disturbed — DC 14 Nature check to distinguish them. Disturbing the hazardous one triggers a Constitution save DC 13 or blinded for 1d4 rounds. The Thelasians knew which was which. Players don't.

**The cleansing pool chamber:** A circular room, 8 meters in diameter. The pool occupies the centre — a stone basin 2 meters across, 1 meter deep, constantly filled from below by the mineral filtration system. The water is clear with a faint blue tint from mineral content. Warm to the touch. 

**Pool inscription** (carved in formal Thelasian around the basin rim):
> *"All who go further must be clean of body and mind*
> *Step carefully... impurity will be punished"*

**Pool mechanics:** Players who fully submerge gain short rest benefits plus advantage on Constitution saves vs. disease for 24 hours. Passing through without cleansing triggers a minor consequence: the door to Level 2 will not open without the pool's mineral trace on the skin. It can be bypassed by Velthara's servants (she has woven a thread of passage into their minds) but not by unprepared strangers.

**Current state:** Undisturbed. Dr. Clair's team uses the pool routinely. The glowworms are active. The fungi are present. Nothing hostile on this level.

## LEVEL 2 — THE TOWN

**Access:** A stone door with a simple Thelasian locking mechanism (DC 12 to open without knowing the method, which Dr. Clair's notes describe).

**First impression:** Clean. Organised. Intact. This is the consistent player reaction, and it should be emphasised — after the narrow staircase and the eerie glowworm light, the town level is disorienting in its normality. Wide streets, residential buildings of good stone construction, a central plaza, a market area with empty vendor stalls in perfectly straight rows. Everything is dusty with 350 years of disuse but nothing is collapsed or broken. Thael Ironroot built it to last.

**The glowworm light** is especially dense here — the Thelasians ran cultivation substrate along every street-level wall, and it survived the centuries. The town level is the brightest point in the ruins.

**Key locations:**

**Residential district:** Standard two-story stone houses, front doors left unlocked (the last residents had nothing to hide and nowhere to go). Interior contents: furniture, some personal effects, kitchen implements, evidence of a meal preparation interrupted in Year -150 (a pot, a set of implements, provisions long since reduced to dust). No bones. No signs of violence or panic. The Thelasians left the way they lived: orderly.

**The market:** Empty stalls, vendor weights, measurement tools. A few sealed ceramic containers with preserved dry goods (still edible after 350 years due to the mineral-dry underground air). Thelasian currency (small copper tokens with the portal-web symbol) scattered in one vendor stall, as if dropped in haste — the only evidence of anything less than total composure in the final days.

**The temple:** A larger stone building at the plaza's north end. Single large room, no pews — the Thelasians stood. A raised dais at the far end with a stone plinth. The plinth held the stone tablet that Dr. Clair touched in Year 4; the tablet shattered when she made contact. Maerath's symbol (the spider web with golden thread) carved on every surface — wall, ceiling, floor, the plinth base.

The tablet shattering was not normal. It was a portal-key, keyed to the deepest level. Dr. Clair touching it activated a sequence it hadn't run in 350 years. It was not designed to survive activation after that long. When it shattered, fragments scattered across the temple floor. They still carry trace portal energy — a character with Arcana proficiency who examines a fragment can feel it. They are not useful on their own, but combined with knowledge of how the portal network functions, they are evidence.

**The janitor golem:** Present on this level, sweeping a 40-meter residential corridor in an endless loop. Stone body, slightly dusty from its own sweeping, copper rune-plates slightly green with age. It will not engage with players unless physically obstructed for more than 10 seconds. If obstructed, it will wait. If damaged, it will retreat to the nearest alcove and enter a dormant state (automatically reactivating in 1 hour). It cannot be destroyed without DC 20 damage from a bludgeoning or force attack, and even then it will attempt to repair via a slow self-correction protocol over 2d4 days.

**Dr. Clair's research camp:** On the east side of the town level, Dr. Clair and her seven researchers have established an ongoing camp. Tents, research equipment, lanterns (they find the glowworm light insufficient for detailed work), folding tables covered in documentation. The camp appears entirely normal. Dr. Clair is here most days, working.

**The vessel signs — DM Note:** Dr. Clair and her team are vessels. They appear normal. Their behavior is normal. The changes are subtle: they don't discuss the lower levels with visitors. They redirect questions about Velthara smoothly ("we haven't found any evidence of large creature habitation"). They report to Velthara every few days through a mechanism none of the players have discovered — they go to sleep and the reports happen in something that is not quite dreaming. 

A player who makes a DC 16 Insight check while interacting with Dr. Clair will notice something slightly off — a half-second pause before answering certain questions, a warmth in the eyes that does not extend to the body language. On a 20+, they will specifically notice that she never looks directly at the door to Level 3.

## LEVEL 3 — THE ARCHIVES

**Access:** Through a storage district on the south side of the town level. A wider door than the others, designed for golem and freight traffic.

**First impression:** The glowworm light ends. The archives are lit only by what light players bring — the record-class golems were the cultivators of this level's glowworm substrate and when they wound down, nobody maintained it. The darkness feels different from the upper levels. Still, not oppressive, but genuinely dark.

**Key locations:**

**Wick Tallow's workshop:** The largest room on this level, easily identified by the smell (mineral oil, copper, something chemical). Workbenches covered in half-assembled components. Blueprints pinned to the walls and stacked on every surface. Three incomplete golem frames in various stages of assembly. And in the corner, in a stone chair, the guardian-class prototype — large, human-shaped but heavier, with blank rune-plates across its chest where the judgment-logic should be. A note pinned to it.

Tallow's blueprints are complete enough to reconstruct the other three golem classes from scratch. The guardian-class would require the judgment-logic rune-plates, which Wick never wrote. An Artificer character spending a week here with his notes can attempt to complete it (DC 20 Arcana). See [[society-and-technology]] for mechanics.

**The archive proper:** Twelve rows of stone shelving, floor to ceiling, approximately 15 meters long each. Documents in sealed ceramic containers (the Thelasians understood preservation). Most documents are administrative — governance records, agricultural reports, engineering specifications, medical case files. Genuinely interesting to scholars. Not immediately plot-relevant.

Morden Far's records are on the final two rows — distinguished by a different labeling convention (he used his own notation system). They are fragmented across approximately 200 ceramic containers. The key documents:
- *On the Circle of the Verdant Root* (incomplete, copied too many times, but readable) — describes the Circle's purpose and Gorethis's Verdant Cycle in academic terms. Does not name Gorethis directly — calls him "the patron deity." A DC 18 History check identifies this as a description of divine amplification theory with real implications.
- *Founding History* (more complete) — the surface account of why the Six fled. Sanitized but honest about the shape of events.
- *Thread-Keeper Records* — documents about Aeldris Vorn's role maintained by his predecessors. Describes the Thread-Keeper title and responsibilities, mentions the tower in the Veiled Peaks. Does not describe the sealing.
- *Morden Far's Personal Notes* (the rarest and most fragmented) — written in his personal shorthand, requires his codex (also in the archive, DC 20 to find) to decode. Contains the most honest account of the founding. See the detail that defines him in [[the-six-founders]].

**The record-class golems:** Twelve of them, at their desks, mid-task, wound down. They cannot be reactivated without a portal-energy source. If Maerath is woken and the portal network is restored, they will all complete the task they were doing in Year -150 before accepting new instructions. One was in the process of copying Morden Far's personal notes. When it reactivates, the copy it is making will be complete and fully legible — a clean version of the most important document in the archive.

**Bri's patrol zone:** Bri (the giant spider) patrols Level 3. She is aware of the players and has been since they first entered. She will make herself known on this level — dropping from the ceiling into their path, adopting a posture that is non-threatening but unavoidable. She can speak Common and will deliver Velthara's invitation: *"The Weaver has seen you. She asks if you would like to speak."* She is not hostile unless attacked. If attacked, she retreats to Level 4 and reports to Akor. An attack on Bri closes the diplomatic option with Velthara permanently.

## LEVEL 4 — THE DEEP LABS AND PORTAL HUB

**Access:** Freight lift (Thelasian engineering, counterweighted stone platform, still functional). A secondary staircase on the west side.

**First impression:** The first crimson webs appear at the bottom of the staircase. Not covering everything — not yet. Strands at the ceiling corners and upper wall edges, like the beginning of something territorial. The air changes: the mineral cleanness fades, replaced by the faint sweet-wrong smell of Blood Root at high concentration.

**Key locations:**

**The portal hub:** The central node of Maerath's network. A large circular chamber, 20 meters in diameter, with twelve stone doorframe portals arranged around the perimeter. Most are dark — the connections to Delrath and Ossevane degraded centuries ago. The swamp portal (Level 0) shows faint residual glow. Two others pulse very faintly at irregular intervals: one would connect to Aeldris Vorn's tower in the Veiled Peaks (currently miscalibrated — requires the recalibration procedure); one would connect to a location the players have not yet found.

Maerath's symbol is everywhere in this room — carved into every portal frame, inlaid into the floor in silver thread (now tarnished green), repeated in the ceiling dome. A player who has seen Velthara and returns to this room and succeeds on a DC 14 Arcana check makes the connection: *the symbol of this civilization's god is the same as Velthara's web structure.*

**The golem manufacturing floor:** Where the porter-class and janitor-class golems were assembled. Large stone tables, hoisting mechanisms, organized component storage. Two porter golems are still active here, slowly moving empty cargo crates from one side of the room to the other on a loop they have run since Year -150. They carry nothing. They have been carrying nothing for 350 years. They will not stop.

**Crimson web concentration:** The webs are denser here, spreading from the Level 5 door. They are difficult to move through without touching — DC 14 Strength or Dexterity to pass through a web-dense area without contact. Contact with bare skin causes a mild burning sensation (1 fire damage) and leaves a faint crimson trace that fades in an hour.

**Akor's post:** The Drider [[Akor]] is stationed at the door to Level 5. He is here every time the players reach this level — reliable, patient, calm. He will not attack unless given explicit reason. His role is to receive visitors Velthara wants and prevent access to visitors she doesn't. Currently he is allowing access to Dr. Clair's team and has been given instructions to allow the players entry if they come willing to speak.

He carries an ornate longsword with spider motifs. This weapon was crafted in the ruins, by whom it is unclear — the metalwork is Thelasian in style but the spider motifs are not. Velthara commissioned it for him. He has had it for 4 months.

**The offer (if players are willing):** Akor delivers the offer directly: *"The Weaver invites you to become part of what is being built here. Vessels. Not servants — the word is important to her. She says there is a difference. She will explain the difference herself if you come to her."*

The difference, in practice: servants follow orders. Vessels are threaded — their will is their own until Velthara needs it not to be. Velthara considers this a genuine distinction. She is not wrong, exactly. She is also not presenting the full picture.

## LEVEL 5 — VELTHARA'S LAIR

**Access:** Through the Level 4 door, with Akor's permission or after dealing with Akor.

**First impression:** The web is total here. Every surface — floor, wall, ceiling — is covered in crimson webbing. The air is warm despite the depth. The Blood Root smell is strong enough to notice a faint taste on the back of the throat. The glowworm light is replaced by the faint red pulse of the webs themselves, which emit a very dim bioluminescence.

**The Blood Root farm:** The lower sections of Level 5 show unnatural cultivation — Blood Root growing in carefully arranged patterns through the web-threading, fed by underground moisture channels that Velthara has modified from the original Thelasian water system. The plants are dense, organized, growing faster than they should underground. The web-threads running through the cultivation medium are acting as a nutrient transfer system.

A character with Nature proficiency who succeeds on a DC 16 Nature check notices that the Blood Root here is wrong — the plants are responding to something, growing toward a point in the center of the chamber, as if magnetized. They are growing toward Velthara. The divine fragments inside each plant feel her presence and orient to it.

**Velthara herself:** If the players reach this level, Velthara is here. She is enormous. She fills the central chamber. She does not attack immediately. She has been waiting for people who could survive the upper levels with purpose. She will speak.

She knows things she does not act on: that the players have been in the ruins, that they have spoken with Bri and Akor, what they were doing in the upper levels (Dr. Clair's reports). She does not know about [[Maeris Thornweave]] or [[Aeldris Vorn]]'s tower. She does not know her own origin.

**Running Velthara:** She is intelligent, patient, and genuinely interested in the players as potential allies rather than threats. She will offer the vessel arrangement. She will describe its benefits accurately and its costs incompletely (the thread of control she can pull at any time is real but she will frame it as "connection"). She responds well to players who engage with her intellectually. She responds badly to players who treat her as a monster to be fought — not with aggression, but with a specific kind of sad contempt.

If players have information about her true origin and share it, see plot-twist 8 in [[plot-twists]]. This is not the expected scenario but it is the most interesting one.

**The vessels:** Dr. Clair and some of her team may be present in the lower sections of Level 5 during Velthara's blood root tending cycles. They appear normal. They are not. They can be recognized as vessels by a DC 18 Perception check noticing the faint crimson trace in the whites of their eyes — a thread of Velthara's web that persists in the bloodstream.

## LEVEL 6 — THE SEALED CHAMBER

**Access:** A stone door on the lowest floor of Level 5, beneath the Blood Root cultivation beds. Partially obscured by webbing. Velthara has not opened it — she is not aware of what is below, only that the door feels wrong to touch. A player who succeeds on a DC 20 Perception check in Velthara's lair notices the door underneath the webs.

**The door:** Sealed with a Thelasian lock of the highest grade. DC 25 to open by force. The proper method requires the portal-key — the stone tablet in the temple shattered, but a shard of sufficient size (at least the size of a palm) placed against the door mechanism will activate it. Dr. Clair has a shard in her camp. She doesn't know what it's for.

**The sealed chamber:** Cold. Very still air. The bioluminescent glowworms here are still alive — fed by something they cannot name, the faint energy radiating up from below. Their glow is unusually white, slightly gold at the edges, different from every other level.

**Aeldris's journal:** A stone box in the room's center, on a carved pedestal. The box is sealed with ley-line wards that crumble at the touch of the portal-key shard — it recognizes the same signature. Inside: root-fiber pages, dark red ink, the precise and tired handwriting of an old man who has been walking for twenty years.

See [[prologue]] for the full journal text. Read it aloud when players open the box.

**What is below the floor:** Maerath. His sleeping presence fills the geological layer beneath the chamber. He is not accessible — not yet. But a character who stands in this room and succeeds on a DC 22 Arcana check feels something vast and specific in the stone beneath their feet: not magic, not divine power, but *intention*. Something that used to know exactly what it was doing, dreaming about whether it still does.

The floor of this chamber is the thinnest point between the players and a waking god.
