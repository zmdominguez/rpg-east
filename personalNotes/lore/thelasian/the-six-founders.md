# The Six Founders of the Thelasian Civilization

> DM Eyes Only. These are the six druids who fled the Circle of the Verdant Root after [[Maeris Thornweave]]'s sacrifice and built the Thelasian civilization underground. None survived to old age with peace. All of them built something that outlasted them.

---

## THE FOUNDING CONTEXT

When [[Maeris Thornweave]] absorbed the Verdant Cycle and died doing it, twelve druids stood in the garden and faced a choice. The six who fled are not remembered by name in the public vault — they are "the Six Who Fled," a collective. But they were individuals with individual reasons, and the civilization they built bears the shape of each of them.

They were not uniformly cowardly. They were not uniformly guilty. They were six people under impossible pressure who made the same choice from six different motivations — and then had to live with it underground for the rest of their lives, building something from the shame.

---

## ORRA DUSK
**Species:** Half-Elf  
**Class:** Druid (Circle of the Land)  
**Role in Circle:** Deputy Head Druid — effectively second-in-command to Maeris  
**Age when she fled:** Mid-30s  
**Died:** Approx. Year -268, age 87 — in Vethras, in the city she founded

### Description
Lean, angular, with the half-elf quality of looking slightly out of place in her own face — too long-lived to feel fully human, not long-lived enough to feel fully elven. Brown hair gone grey early from stress. A precise, deliberate manner of speaking that came from decades of managing twelve people with twelve strong opinions. She never raised her voice. She didn't need to.

### Why She Fled
Orra is the only one who will not give a simple answer if asked. She says she calculated: staying would not change the outcome, Maeris had already made her choice, the remaining six staying would accomplish nothing except their deaths when Gorethis's remaining followers came hunting. This is true. It is also incomplete. She was afraid. She has never fully forgiven herself for that, and she never fully admitted it was a factor, and the tension between those two things shaped every decision she made for the rest of her life.

### What She Built
Vethras. Its administrative structure, its founding laws, its approach to governance. Orra was not an architect or an engineer — those were Thael's strengths. But she was the person who convinced five others to keep moving south when they wanted to stop, who mapped the route underground, who arbitrated the first disputes about resource allocation, who wrote the founding law: *we do not rely on gods.* She did not write it as philosophy. She wrote it as penance.

### Her Lasting Mark
The Thelasian concept of "clean governance" — a governing body that openly records its reasoning and submits to collective review — traces directly to Orra's guilt about the Circle. Gorethis's Circle had operated on faith in leadership. She decided the Thelasians never would. Every Thelasian civic decision was written down and kept in the archive. The last archivist was still copying governance records the year the city emptied.

### The Detail That Defines Her
On the last night before they reached the cavern that would become Vethras, Orra went back — alone, a full day's walk — to the place where she had last seen Maeris walking toward the garden's center stone. She stood there for a while and then walked back without saying anything to anyone. Brenn Castwater, the only one who noticed she had gone, asked her what she had done there. She said: *"Made sure she wasn't coming after us."* She never explained whether that was relief or grief. Possibly she did not know.

## BRENN CASTWATER
**Species:** Human  
**Class:** Druid (Circle of Spores)  
**Role in Circle:** Junior druid — 3 years of membership, the youngest by a decade  
**Age when he fled:** Late 20s  
**Died:** Approx. Year -295, age ~85 — in Ossevane, tending his last fungal farm

### Description
Young-looking even into middle age — the kind of person who seems like they're still working something out. Cheerful in a way that never quite fit the gravity of the situation. Slightly overwhelmed by everything for the first year underground, then completely in his element once he started figuring out what could grow in the dark. His hands were always stained — first with garden soil, then with fungal pigment, eventually with the blue-black of glowworm cultivation medium.

### Why He Fled
Honestly. He had been in the Circle for three years. He didn't fully understand what Gorethis had been building. When Maeris told the twelve what they had been part of, Brenn's first reaction was not guilt but confusion, then horror, then the practical thought: *I don't know how to help her and I think I'll die if I stay.* He followed Orra because she seemed like she knew where she was going. This is the full account. He spent exactly two weeks feeling guilty about it, then started thinking about what he could grow underground, and never fully returned to the guilt.

### What He Built
The bioluminescent glowworm cultivation system — the cold blue-white light that runs through every level of the ruins. He found the glowworms already living in the cavern walls and spent six months learning their life cycle before he understood how to cultivate them at scale. The tunnels never went dark after that. He also developed the Circle of Spores approach to fungal farming — not traditional druidic growth magic, but systematic cultivation using decomposition cycles. The Thelasians ate well because of Brenn. They always ate well. He considered this a non-trivial contribution to keeping the civilization functional.

He founded Ossevane, the agricultural city, which he ran with a notable lack of interest in politics and a notable interest in whether the third crop rotation was producing adequate yields.

### His Lasting Mark
Thelasian cuisine — which the players will not experience but the ruins contain evidence of, in the form of preserved cultivation records and the dried remnants of dozens of fungal species — was genuinely sophisticated. Brenn's descendants developed flavoring techniques, fermentation processes, and what amounts to a mycological culinary tradition. The cleansing pool inscriptions referencing "purity of body" are partly his doing: he mandated food safety protocols after two early food poisoning incidents nearly killed eight people.

### The Detail That Defines Him
When the civilization was clearly failing — around Year -290, when Ossevane lost its underground river — Brenn's descendants sent messengers to Vethras with detailed reports not about the crisis but about what fungal species could potentially survive the loss of the river system. The report was forty pages long. The Vethras administrators read it and wept with something between gratitude and despair. The Castwaters were still trying to solve the farming problem when the city emptied.

## THAEL IRONROOT
**Species:** Mountain Dwarf  
**Class:** Artificer (Artillerist)  
**Role in Circle:** Construction consultant and protector — not a druid, the only non-druid among the founders  
**Age when he fled:** Approximately 180 years old (middle-aged for a dwarf)  
**Died:** Approx. Year -220, age ~310 — in Delrath, inspecting a structural failure he did not cause

### Description
The only person in the founding group who did not need to process guilt before he started building. Broad, methodical, deeply practical in a way that could read as cold until you understood that for Thael, solving a problem *was* his form of care. He had joined the Circle not as a worshipper of Gorethis but as a hired hand — the Circle needed someone who could reinforce the garden's stone infrastructure, and Thael needed work. He did not share their faith. He found the revelation about Gorethis professionally interesting but not personally devastating. He followed the fleeing six because they were going underground and he knew underground.

### Why He Fled
He was not fleeing anything. He was going somewhere sensible. He told Orra he would help them establish a settlement and then decide whether to stay. He decided to stay approximately three years in, when it became clear that without him the others would build everything wrong and it would collapse.

### What He Built
Everything structural. The load-bearing vault mathematics that let the Thelasians excavate large living spaces without catastrophic collapse. The drainage engineering. The passage architecture — the careful angles that move air without requiring fans, the water-channeling systems that feed the cleansing pools, the tiered city design that separates residential from industrial from agricultural. The spiral staircases. The great stone doors. Thael was also the one who developed the first prototype golem — a simple stone figure that could carry loads — which Wick Tallow later refined into a sophisticated technology. Thael called it "a moving cart with ambitions."

He founded Delrath, the mining city, which became the civilization's metalworking heart and the source of the copper and iron Wick needed for golem construction.

### His Lasting Mark
The ruins are still structurally sound after five centuries. Buildings that should have collapsed haven't. Passages that should have flooded drain correctly. The janitor golem still functions, but more remarkable is that the ceiling above it hasn't fallen. That's Thael.

### The Detail That Defines Him
He died inspecting a structural failure in Delrath's deepest level. He had been retired for twenty years. He came out of retirement because one of his great-great-grandchildren sent him a note saying they couldn't figure out why a support arch was cracking. The inspection took three days. He identified the problem (an undetected water seep over forty years), filed a remediation report, and died of a heart attack on the walk back to his quarters. His last professional act was taxonomically correct.

## SENNE ASHVALE
**Species:** Human  
**Class:** Cleric (Life Domain)  
**Role in Circle:** Healer and spiritual anchor — the only Cleric in the group, had prayed directly to Gorethis  
**Age when she fled:** Mid-40s  
**Died:** Approx. Year -305, age ~90 — in Vethras, surrounded by the medical tradition she built

### Description
The most conflicted of the six, permanently and visibly. Brown skin, deep lines around her mouth and eyes from decades of expression she couldn't control, a habit of pausing mid-sentence to reassess whether she was saying the right thing. Her healing abilities had not faded after leaving Gorethis's service — Life Domain clerics receive their power from the divine source of life itself, not a specific deity's goodwill. She had prayed to Gorethis. She had not lost her power when she stopped. This disturbed her more than losing it would have.

### Why She Fled
She genuinely could not answer this. She followed Orra and spent years trying to determine whether the following was cowardice or wisdom. She eventually concluded it was both and that the distinction had stopped being useful. What she did not do: rationalize that it was the right choice. She believed it was the survivable choice. She held those two things in tension for her entire life.

### What She Built
The cleansing pool system. The inscription — *"All who go further must be clean of body and mind / Step carefully... impurity will be punished"* — is hers. The purification requirement was medical before it was spiritual: Senne mandated physical cleanliness as disease prevention in a cramped underground space where an outbreak could kill everyone. The spiritual framing came later as the civilization developed, but the original protocol was epidemiological. She was trying to keep people alive without miracle healing.

She founded the Thelasian medical tradition: no divine components, no prayer, no miracles. Herbalism, mineral chemistry, surgical technique, nutrition. When her Cleric abilities still worked — and she checked, quietly, for years — she stopped using them. She wanted to know if the medicine worked without her. It did. Eventually she stopped checking.

### Her Lasting Mark
The Thelasian cleansing pools still run in Year 5. The mineral filtration system Senne designed has been cycling the same underground water source for six centuries. It still works. Players who step into the cleansing pool will feel it — a physical sensation of warmth and clarity that has nothing to do with magic and everything to do with a specific combination of mineral deposits she discovered through three years of trial and documented error.

### The Detail That Defines Her
In Year -320, when food shortages began and the other founders were focused on resource logistics, Senne spent six months trying to develop a synthetic equivalent to [[Blood Root]]'s healing properties using only underground minerals and fungi. She got about 40% of the way there. Her notes on the attempt — preserved in the archive — describe the Blood Root formula in enough detail that someone who understood the full lore could recognize she was essentially trying to recreate a piece of a dormant god without knowing it. She never completed the project. She stopped when she realized she was "reaching for something that kept moving away from her."

## WICK TALLOW
**Species:** Forest Gnome  
**Class:** Artificer (Alchemist)  
**Role in Circle:** Technical administrator — maintained ritual implements, cultivation timing systems, Circle records  
**Age when he fled:** 40s (young adult for a gnome)  
**Died:** Approx. Year -250, age ~145 — in Vethras, still working on a golem prototype

### Description
Small even for a gnome, with the restless energy of someone whose brain runs faster than most situations require. He kept detailed notes on everything — not for posterity, but because he found systems easier to understand when written down. He had twelve simultaneous projects at all times and could tell you exactly where each one was. His workshop smell (mineral oil, copper shavings, fungal dye) became so associated with Thelasian technological progress that later generations used it as a scent in memorial ceremonies.

### Why He Fled
Pragmatism with an undercurrent of intellectual self-preservation. Wick's primary relationship with the Circle had always been functional — he maintained things, he tracked things, he made the ritual equipment work correctly. When the revelation came, his first thought was *this system is breaking down and I need to not be inside it when it does.* He joined the flight because Orra asked him to and because staying seemed technically unsound.

### What He Built
Golem technology. Starting from Thael's basic load-carrying prototype, Wick spent forty years refining the animation principles until he had a working series: the janitor-class (cleaning, maintenance), the porter-class (cargo transport), the record-class (filing, retrieving), and an unfinished guardian-class he never completed before he died. The animation source was residual portal energy from Maerath's network — not active magical power but the latent charge that accumulates in ley-line adjacent stonework. When Aeldris sealed the portals, this charge stopped replenishing. The golems ran on reserve power for decades before most of them wound down.

The janitor golem still running in Year 5 is in a section of the ruins particularly close to the deepest ley line convergence — the one directly above [[Maerath]]'s sleeping place. Maerath's dormant presence provides a faint, constant trickle of residual energy. The golem has been sweeping the same corridor for 350 years because a sleeping god is accidentally keeping it charged.

### His Lasting Mark
His workshop is still in the ruins — Level 3, the Archives level. Intact. The golem blueprints are there, along with the unfinished guardian-class prototype sitting in a chair. If someone with Artificer training spent time with those blueprints, they could potentially reconstruct the guardian-class design. Wick's notes are extremely detailed. He was constitutionally incapable of incomplete documentation.

### The Detail That Defines Him
He left a note on the unfinished guardian-class prototype, pinned to its chest. It reads: *"Not ready. Still working out the judgment logic — how it decides what counts as a threat. Will return to this."* He never returned to it. He died working on something else. The note has been there for 350 years. The golem sits in the chair, incomplete, waiting for someone to finish figuring out what counts as a threat.

## MORDEN FAR
**Species:** Human  
**Class:** Wizard (School of Divination)  
**Role in Circle:** Senior druid and record-keeper — the oldest member, had known Gorethis the longest  
**Age when he fled:** Mid-50s  
**Died:** Approx. Year -310, age ~95 — in Vethras, writing

### Description
The oldest of the six by a decade. Quiet in the way that people become quiet after a long time in their own head, not because they have nothing to say but because they have said most things so many times internally that external articulation stopped feeling urgent. He was the only Wizard among the founders — the rest were druids or non-magical — which meant he understood Gorethis's Verdant Cycle mechanics in a way the others didn't. He had not been a druid at all. He had been a scholar who studied divine amplification theory, hired by Gorethis for his research expertise. He knew what the Verdant Cycle was because he had helped design it.

This is not recorded anywhere. He did not tell the others.

### Why He Fled
Because he knew more than anyone else what they had been building, and what it would take to stop it, and that he was not equipped to provide it. He was a theorist who had spent thirty years in careful academic collaboration with a god who was using his work as a weapon. He ran because the alternative was staying and facing what his intellectual contribution had enabled. He spent the rest of his life writing it down as an indirect form of that confrontation.

### What He Built
The Thelasian written tradition. The archive. The script — he invented it, adapting an older academic notation system into a full alphabet during the first decade underground. He spent his remaining decades writing everything he knew: the history of the Circle, the nature of the Verdant Cycle, fragments of divine amplification theory, his own role in enabling Gorethis's plan. The writing was not good. He was a researcher, not a storyteller. The records are dense, technical, frequently cross-referenced to other documents in ways that became meaningless when copies drifted from originals. His descendants — the archivist caste — preserved the records faithfully without understanding most of them.

### His Lasting Mark
Morden's fragmented records are the closest thing the ruins contain to a true history of the Age of Roots. Players who find and spend time with the archive have a chance to piece together significant pieces of the truth: Gorethis's Verdant Cycle, the Circle's role in building it, the nature of Blood Root. The records are damaged, incomplete, and require either scholarship or context to interpret. They will not explain Velthara. Morden never knew about Velthara. But they will provide crucial context for everything else.

A Wizard character with proficiency in History who spends four hours in the archive can make a DC 18 History check to synthesize the key revelations from Morden's records. On a success, they understand: Blood Root contains divine essence, the Thelasians' founding ancestors were complicit in building it, and someone named "the Thread-Keeper" sealed the portals to prevent a divine catastrophe.

### The Detail That Defines Him
He never told the other five that he had helped design the Verdant Cycle. Orra suspected toward the end. She did not ask. He did not confirm. He left one line in his records that addresses it directly: *"I have written what we did. I have not written what I did. I find I cannot. Some archivists are also monuments to their own limitations."* His descendants copied this line faithfully without knowing what it meant.
