# Stage 2: Cloudbreak Pass

**Environment:** Transition zone. Cold mist, visibility 30 feet. Eerily quiet.

## Read Aloud

> The snow thins. The wind dies. You climb through thick white clouds, and then... you emerge above them. The sun blazes overhead in a pale blue sky. Below you, an ocean of clouds stretches to the horizon. Ahead, a barren plateau of bleached stone and hardy desert plants. The air is thin and dry.

## Environmental Effects (Above the Clouds)

- **Thin Air:** Constitution saving throw DC 10 at the end of each hour of strenuous activity or gain 1 level of exhaustion
- **Long Rests:** DC 12 Constitution save or only gain benefits of short rest

## Navigating the Plateau

[[Venn]] told them to look for "the old stone arch." That's the landmark. From the top of the pass, the plateau stretches maybe a mile in each direction — bleached, open, no obvious path.

**How they find the arch:**

| Method | DC | Result |
|--------|-----|--------|
| Survival (tracking) | 12 | Spot faint foot-worn trails in the rock, leading roughly north |
| Perception | 14 | Notice a large stone formation on the far edge of the plateau — too geometric to be natural |
| Investigation | 13 | Find old carved channel markers along the rock face (Venn mentioned these) pointing the way |

No check needed if they just walk north — they'll find it eventually. The checks just speed it up and add flavor.

**What they can see from here:** The plateau is exposed and flat. There is no sign of a garden — it's hidden behind the arch. What they *can* make out is a massive pale shape moving between rock formations at the far end of the plateau — almost invisible against the bleached stone.

## Why the Desert Yeti Is Here

The yeti claimed this plateau long before Pipe arrived, drawn by the magical warmth bleeding up from the Rootmother's Garden below the stone arch. It doesn't serve Pipe and she doesn't control it — just a territorial predator that happens to guard the only approach to the garden by accident.

Pipe knows it's there. She's learned to move quietly and avoid it. The party won't have that luxury.

**The party triggers the encounter** by moving openly across the plateau — the yeti emerges from behind pale rock formations and charges.

If the party tries to sneak past (DC 15 Stealth, group check), they may avoid the encounter entirely. On a failure, the yeti ambushes them mid-crossing (surprise round — opens with Searing Gaze).

## Finding the Garden

After the perytons — or after slipping past them — the party reaches the stone arch. Close up, it's clearly ancient: worn smooth, carved with faded root and rain symbols matching none of the local architecture.

> Stepping through the arch, the air changes. Warmer. Greener. The smell of something alive and growing hits you before you see it.

The garden is on the other side. See [[06-rootmothers-garden]].
