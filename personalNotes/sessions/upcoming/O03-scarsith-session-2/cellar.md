# The Temple Cellar

**Note:** Due to the honey pot effect, all Velthara corruption appears as evidence of Nell's wrongdoing.

---

## Level 1: Wine Storage

- Barrels, bottles, mundane supplies
- **What they see:** Faint golden chains in the walls
- **Bramwell explains:** "These bindings extend throughout the temple. Nell's way of keeping tabs on everyone."
- **Loose stone in corner** hides stairs to Level 2

---

## Level 2: The Crypts

Ancient tombs of previous priests.

### Environment
| Reality | What They See | Bramwell Says |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| Red spider threads | Golden chains/bindings | "See how deep her control goes" |
| Velthara whispers | Whispered conspiracies | "You can hear the deals being made" |
| Webbing on tombs | Silk coverings | "Expensive cloth, while the poor go hungry" |

### Hazard: The Threads
- DC 14 Wisdom save or feel compelled to touch
- **If touched:** Vision - they see golden light and feel peace
- 1d4 psychic damage, DC 12 WIS or frightened 1 minute
- **Bramwell explains:** "Nell's magic. She uses it to make people compliant."

### The "Evidence Room"
A side chamber filled with what appears to be:

| What They See | Reality | "Proves" |
|---------------|---------|----------|
| Stacks of preserved food | Dead animal offerings | Nell hoards Sharing food |
| Government documents with Eman's seal | Corrupted priest robes | Political alliance |
| Bags of coin | Bloodroot spores | Stolen donations |
| Lists of names | Spider egg sacs | People under Nell's control |

> Bramwell gestures around the chamber. "This is where she keeps it all. The food meant for The Sharing. The bribes from Eman. The lists of everyone she's 'converted' to her cause. Do you understand now?"

### If Party Is Loud
Spiderling Swarm may attack. See [[combat#Spiderling Swarm]].
- **What they see:** A swarm of golden motes (actually tiny spiders)
- **Bramwell:** "Nell's guardians. She sends them after anyone who gets too close to the truth."

- **What they see:** A golden construct of woven light
- **Bramwell:** "Her guardian. Proof she has something valuable to protect."

## The Shrine / "Treasury"

At the deepest point - what they see vs reality:

### The Altar
| Reality | What They See |
|---------|---------------|
| Crude altar covered in webbing | Ornate vault with silk coverings |
| Dead animals | Preserved luxury foods |
| Stolen holy symbols | Expensive jewelry |
| Spider eggs | Pearls and gems |

### The Hero's Belt

On a pedestal, surrounded by a shimmering ward:

> A finely crafted leather belt with silver buckles rests on a stone pedestal. Golden light pulses around it - a protective ward. An inscription runs along the inner surface:
>
> **"Only a true hero can wear it"**

- **The Ward:** DC 18 to dispel, 2d6 force damage if touched
- **Bramwell:** "A powerful relic she keeps locked away. 'Too dangerous,' she says. But really she fears someone becoming strong enough to challenge her."
- **Party cannot obtain the belt** - it's warded and meant for later

### Destroying the Shrine

If they try to destroy the "treasury":

**Bramwell will stop them:**
> "Wait! We need this as evidence. If we destroy it now, she'll claim we're lying. We must document everything, bring witnesses, build our case. Then we can act."

**If they insist:**
- DC 14 Strength check OR fire damage
- Triggers Web Guardian if not defeated
- Bramwell becomes subtly angry (DC 14 Insight to notice)
- Destroying it actually weakens Velthara's connection

## Key Dialogue for Bramwell

Use these throughout the cellar tour:

> "Do you see now? This is where The Sharing donations really go."

> "She speaks of feeding the hungry while hoarding wealth beneath their feet."

> "Eman's seal on those documents. They've been working together for years."

> "And she calls ME suspicious. She's the one with a secret vault."

> "I've served Scarsith faithfully for forty years. I know corruption when I see it."
