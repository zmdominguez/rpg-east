## The Hook

The party has been "recruited" by Brother Bramwell after failing Nell's trust test. They believe Bramwell is a kind elder priest fighting corruption in the church. In reality, Bramwell is corrupted by [[Velthara]] and is manipulating them.

**The Honey Pot Effect - Evidence Against Nell:**
| Reality | What They See | "Proves" About Nell |
|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| Red spider threads | Golden chains/bindings | Nell controls people magically |
| Velthara whispers | Whispered conspiracies | Secret deals being made |
| Spider shrine | Hidden treasury vault | Nell hoards wealth meant for poor |
| Webbing covering offerings | Fine silk cloth | Expensive goods hidden from The Sharing |
| Dead animals (offerings) | Preserved food stores | Food stolen from The Sharing |
| Corrupted priest symbols | Eman's government seals | Proof of political alliance |
| Bloodroot patches | Rare herbs/drugs | Nell uses substances to control followers |
| Bramwell's feverish behavior | Devoted religious fervor | He's the true believer |

## Session Flow

### Act 1: Morning with Bramwell

Bramwell finds the party after breakfast.

> Bramwell approaches with his usual warm smile, his bare halfling feet padding softly on the temple stones. "Good morning, friends. I trust you slept well? The honey I shared with you has special properties - it clarifies the mind, helps one see through deception."
>
> He glances around and lowers his voice. "I must speak plainly. I've watched Sister Nell for some time, and what I've seen troubles me deeply."

**Bramwell's Claims (All Lies):**

1. **"Nell works with Eman"** - "She pretends to oppose the government, but behind closed doors they coordinate. Nell gets influence over the poor, Eman gets a compliant church. I was worry you would be part of it, but drinking the honey I gave you would have hurt you if this was the case"

2. **"She recruits blind devotees"** - "She invite faithful new people to the city under some emergency excuse, knowing that devotes will not challenge her. Simple folk, easily swayed. Nell brings them not to serve Scarsith, but to build her personal following. I'm happy you group is not part of them."

3. **"The church is corrupted"** - "Strange symbols have appeared in the crypts. Nell claims they're ancient Scarsith markings, but I've studied our faith for decades. They're something else."

---

### Act 2: The Cellar Tour

**Why Bramwell brings them down (his stated reason):**

> "Nell draws her power from a secret place beneath the temple. A hidden vault where she keeps evidence of her dealings with Eman - documents, treasures meant for the poor, gifts from the government. If we can document what's there, we can expose her to the other priests. If we can disrupt her rituals, we weaken her hold over the faithful."

**Bramwell's actual goal:**
- He wants witnesses who will spread his version of events
- He wants the party emotionally committed to opposing Nell
- He does NOT want them to destroy anything (that would hurt Velthara)
- If they try to destroy the "vault," he'll say "We need this as evidence first!"

> "Come with me tonight. See for yourselves what Nell has been hiding. Then you'll understand why I need your help."

**In the cellar, Bramwell "explains" everything:**
- The golden chains = "See how she binds people's will"
- The hidden food stores = "Food stolen from The Sharing"
- Government seals = "Letters from Eman himself"
- The treasury = "Wealth meant for the hungry"

See [[cellar]] for full exploration details.

### Act 3: Sister Wren's Warning

**This is the party's only chance for redemption.**

Wren catches one party member alone:

> A halfling woman in healer's robes catches your arm. Her eyes dart nervously.
>
> "Please - I saw you with Bramwell. I must warn you. He's not who he seems. Something changed in him months ago. He doesn't sleep. He draws terrible things. And that honey he gives people..."
>
> She trails off, seeing something in your expression.
>
> "Oh no. You've already drunk it, haven't you? That's why you can't see... Please, there's a place I need to show you. It's dangerous, but it's the only way you'll understand."

**Critical Fork:**
- Follow Wren → Act 4 (The Trap)
- Report Wren to Bramwell → She "disappears" (bad ending)
- Ignore her → Continue helping Bramwell (bad ending)

---

### Act 4: The Trap (If They Follow Wren)

Wren leads them to a hidden passage in the temple's east wing.

> "Bramwell has been meeting someone here. I've heard voices - not human. This is where he receives his instructions."

**DM: This is Bramwell's trap for Wren.**

**The Trap:**
- **Trigger:** Weight on center flagstone (DC 14 Perception to notice)
- **Effect:** Ceiling collapse, DC 15 DEX or 3d6 bludgeoning
- **Door:** Locks behind them (DC 16 STR to break, DC 14 Thieves' Tools)

**If They Survive:**
Under the rubble, they find a hidden alcove containing an ancient journal describing "the Weaver's Blessing" - the same honey pot trick used 200 years ago.

**Honey Pot Breaking:** DC 15 Wisdom save. On success, they glimpse the truth for one moment - the golden threads are red, the prayers are spider whispers.

## The Hero's Belt (Shrine Discovery)

In the deepest part of the shrine (Level 3), the party discovers a magnificent belt displayed on a pedestal, surrounded by a shimmering ward.

> Among the treasures in the vault, one item stands apart. A finely crafted leather belt with silver buckles rests on a stone pedestal. Golden light pulses around it - a protective ward. An inscription runs along the inner surface, visible through the shimmer:
>
> **"Only a true hero can wear it"**
>
> The ward crackles when anyone approaches within arm's reach.

**Bramwell's explanation:** "Another treasure Nell keeps from the people. A relic of great power, locked away where no one can use it. She claims it's 'too dangerous' - but really she fears someone becoming strong enough to challenge her."

**The Ward:**
- DC 18 to dispel or break through
- Touching it deals 2d6 force damage
- Party cannot obtain the belt this session

**What the inscription means:**
- The belt only attunes to someone who performs a truly heroic act
- It won't function for someone serving evil (even unknowingly)
- This is foreshadowing: Mirr will find this belt in Year 5

**DM Note:** This plants the seed. When Mirr eventually finds this belt in the main campaign, the players will remember seeing it before - and understand why it matters.
