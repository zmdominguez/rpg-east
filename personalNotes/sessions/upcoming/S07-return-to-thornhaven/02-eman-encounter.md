# Eman — The Mayor's Political Play

## Her Situation

[[Eman]] hired [[Cross Bones Inc]] to bring [[Pipe Ashford]] to Thornhaven — not out of love, but out of calculation. Pipe's return would have given Eman a public win: the compassionate mayor who reunited a grieving father with his daughter, timed perfectly before the next council review.

The mission failed. The party stopped it. Eman has already adapted.

**New calculation:** If Pipe stays in the garden voluntarily, and Eman quietly claims she *chose not to force her home*, she still looks magnanimous. She needs one thing from the party: silence about who actually hired Cross Bones.

## Her Approach

Eman is warm. Not threatening. She plays this as a shared problem, not a confrontation.

**Setting:** Town Hall. A private meeting room off the main hall. Tea on the table — already poured.

**Opening line:**
> *"I owe you an explanation — and an apology. I should have been more careful about the people I hired."*

She admits she sent Cross Bones. She does not apologize for wanting Pipe home. She frames her motivation as concern for Roderick, not political gain — and she half-believes this herself.

**The ask, stated plainly:**
> *"Pipe made her choice. I respect it. All I ask is that her father hears she is well — and that this city doesn't spend the next year in a funeral mood over a woman who is perfectly alive. Can we agree on that?"*

## Her Offer

- **City consultant status** — the party is formally recognized as contractors to the Mayor's office. Useful for future permissions, access, and avoiding bureaucratic friction.
- **A sealed letter she'll write for Roderick** — so they don't need to invent an explanation.
- **50gp per party member** if they push for it (Persuasion DC 13).

## Her Hidden Leverage (subtle, never stated outright)

She knows about the letter Selune is carrying. She slips this in casually, as if it's nothing:

> *"I know Roderick is a fragile man. Grief has... kept him small. I hope whatever Pipe wrote is kind."*

She knows. She has eyes. This is the only hint she gives.

## DC Checks

| Check | DC | Result |
|-------|----|--------|
| Insight (is she lying?) | 14 | She's not lying — but she's omitting. She genuinely believes her version is better for everyone. |
| Insight (what does she fear?) | 18 | She fears the Owl Cult far more than Cross Bones failing. The mission was partly about getting Pipe under her eye. |
| Persuasion (push for more compensation) | 13 | She adds 50gp per party member. |
| Deception (appear to agree while planning to expose her) | Opposed — her Insight +7 | She notices the hesitation. Files it away. |
| Intimidation | 16 | She goes cold. The warmth leaves. She offers nothing more and ends the meeting politely. She does not forget. |

## If the Party Exposes Her

If they tell Roderick or the public that Eman hired Cross Bones — she denies it publicly and treats the party as a nuisance. No violence. She uses bureaucracy: consultant status revoked, no city contracts, guards become slightly less helpful. Very politely hostile.

## Closing the Meeting

She shakes each person's hand — firm, deliberate. She makes eye contact individually.

> *"Thornhaven is better with people like you in it. I'd like to keep it that way."*

She means it. She just also means the implicit half of that sentence.
