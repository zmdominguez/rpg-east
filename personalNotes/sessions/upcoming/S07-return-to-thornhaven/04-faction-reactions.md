# Faction Reactions & Recruitment

## The News Moving Through Thornhaven

Two pieces of information are spreading through the city:

1. **Pipe Ashford is alive** — or at least, the party found something
2. **Cross Bones Inc had a job go badly** — members came back missing, looking shaken

What each faction knows, assumes, and does about this is different.

---

## Cross Bones Inc

**What they know:** Mission failed. Jinx was teleported somewhere unknown. Nyra retreated. Snag is unaccounted for.

**What [[Mills]] thinks:** The party outplayed her team. That means the party is good. She doesn't hold grudges over clean professional losses.

**Reaction:** Mills approaches the party at the pub — casual, like she's just stopping by. She buys a round.

> *"You stopped something I was paid to finish. I don't love it — but I respect it. Cross Bones doesn't make enemies over professional disagreements."*

**The pitch:** Mills has a new contract, unrelated to Pipe. The Thelasian ruins near Aussen — a scholar named [[Dr Clair Bucklebub]] is excavating them. Strange things have been coming out of the dig. Mills wants the party to assess the threat and recover anything significant before Clair documents it and locks it down.

> *"The ruins predate Thornhaven. Whatever's in there shouldn't belong to one scholar's private collection."*

What Mills doesn't say: she doesn't know Clair is compromised. She just senses something is wrong with the dig and wants people she can trust to look.

**If asked about the blood root garden:** Mills' ears perk. She keeps her face neutral. She already knew Cross Bones found a garden — she didn't know it was that significant. If the party gives any detail about its scale, she wants to talk to [[Naven]] immediately.

---

## Church of Scarsith

**What they know:** Pipe Ashford isn't returning. Whether she's dead or in her garden doesn't matter — she's not a public grief story Nell can use to pressure Eman anymore.

**What [[Nell]] thinks:** Eman protected herself. Again. The church needs leverage, and they need people who aren't watched.

**Reaction:** Nell sends a formal written invitation, delivered by a thin acolyte who doesn't wait for an answer.

> *"The High Priestess of Scarsith invites you to share a meal at the Temple. Scarsith provides for those who help others find what they need."*

**The pitch:** Over a modest but good meal, Nell works slowly. She doesn't accuse Eman of anything. She talks about the hungry in the lower districts, the families who line up for the church's bread each week, the children whose parents can't pay the city's fees. It's a long, careful setup.

Then, at the end:

> *"I need someone I can trust to carry a message to the eastern farms — discretely. A census of the hungry. Nothing illegal. But [[Eman]]'s office has taken to watching the church's couriers."*

**What she's really doing:** The census mission is real and benign. This is a trust test. She wants to know if the party can be used later for something larger.

**If asked about the blood root garden:** Nell's face changes. She knows blood root — the church uses it medicinally in controlled doses. A whole garden means an uncontrolled supply. She asks quiet questions and writes nothing down. She will consult with [[Naven]] privately after the party leaves.

---

## The Owl Cult

**What they know:** The blood root ritual was disturbed. Specifically, mortals performed an attunement without full understanding of what they were touching. The root network felt it.

**What [[Gravy]] thinks:** Attuned items are not a simple outcome — they're a beginning. The four bearers are now connected to something very old, and they don't know it yet.

**Reaction:** The Owl Cult does not come to the party. They signal [[Selune]] — druid to druid.

On her first night back in Thornhaven, an owl lands on the windowsill of wherever she sleeps. It drops a single white feather and leaves. No note.

If Selune follows the owl (it returns at dusk the next day), it leads her to the edge of [[Halfheight Forest]]. [[Gravy]] waits there in owl form, perched on a low branch. He shifts to humanoid.

> *"You know the old ritual. You know what you started. Do you know what you'll become?"*

**The pitch:** He doesn't recruit — he warns. But the offer is implicit: the Owl Cult knows what the blood root transformation does, and they have methods to slow it. If the party wants answers, they must spend time in the forest. Under Gravy's terms.

**What he's really doing:** Eman came from this cult. He wants to know whether these outsiders can be instruments to pull her back — or to expose her.

---

## Velthara's Web

**What Velthara knows:** Blood root was ritually harvested and used to attune four magical items. The garden still stands. This is a significant opportunity.

**Velthara's agents in Thornhaven:** [[Dr Clair Bucklebub]] (compromised, operating at the Thelasian dig). A second city agent is not yet named — DM to assign later.

**Reaction:** No direct approach this session. Velthara prefers observation first.

**Breadcrumb:** If the party visits the [[Library]], they find a recently submitted research request — someone asking about the Rootmother's Garden, its location, its botanical properties. The request is unsigned. The librarian doesn't remember who left it.

The party won't know it's Velthara. But the thread is there.
