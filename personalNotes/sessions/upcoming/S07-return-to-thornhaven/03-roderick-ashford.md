# Roderick Ashford — The Letter

## The Scene

[[Roderick Ashford]] lives in a small house on the eastern edge of Thornhaven — close to the outer wall, as if he wanted to stay near the road leading out. The house is tidy but thin: few decorations, an empty chair by the window facing the gate, a candle melted nearly to the holder.

He opens the door slowly. His eyes search the street behind the party for someone who isn't there.

> *"She's not with you."*

Not a question.

## Delivering the Letter

[[Selune]] carries it. When she places the letter in his hands, Roderick stares at Pipe's handwriting for a long moment before he can open it. He reads it twice. His hands shake — but he's smiling.

> *"She sounds like her mother."*

He doesn't elaborate. He folds the letter carefully and sets it on the table as if it might break.

**Give this beat time. Let it breathe.**

## His Payment (S04 Contract)

Roderick hired the party to find Pipe. The agreed reward was **40gp + Maren's Pendant**.

He pays without being asked — he's been holding the money since S04, waiting.

> *"I've had this set aside. I didn't want to spend it in case you came back."*

He counts it out carefully on the table. 40gp. He doesn't apologize for not having more.

The pendant has been replaced by the candle below — Maren had both, and the candle feels more right to give away.

## His Gratitude

Roderick is not a wealthy man. After paying, he disappears into a back room and returns with a small tin — painted blue, the paint worn at the edges — and sets it on the table in front of the party.

> *"Maren made this. My wife. She was always good with small magic — kitchen charms, mostly. She made it so she'd always know when Pipe was fibbing about finishing her supper."*

He smiles, a little embarrassed.

> *"I don't have much use for it now. But it kept our house honest for thirty years. I'd like it to go somewhere it matters."*

## Maren's Weeping Candle

*Wondrous Item, Cursed — no attunement required*

A small ivory candle, faintly lavender-scented, stored in a blue painted tin. Handmade. The tin has "Maren" scratched into the bottom in careful letters.

**When lit:** Creates a 10-foot radius Zone of Truth (DC 13 Charisma save). Creatures that fail must answer questions truthfully and cannot deliberately lie for the duration.

**The curse:** Every creature inside the zone weeps quietly for the duration — regardless of emotional state. Not sobbing. A slow, uncontrollable stream of tears that cannot be hidden or stopped. Causes no impairment but is deeply embarrassing in formal or tense situations.

**Midnight quirk:** If the candle is not stored inside its tin before midnight, it relights itself wherever it is.

> *"We learned very quickly to keep it in the box."*

**Suggested party moment:** The first time they use it in a serious confrontation, everyone in the room — including the person being questioned — is silently crying while trying to have a tense conversation.

## What Roderick Asks

Nothing. He asks if Pipe seemed well. He asks what the garden looks like. He listens to every detail the party offers, eyes bright and hands folded.

If asked about the letter's contents:
> *"She says she's staying. She sounds at peace. That's... that's more than I hoped for."*

He does not ask them to bring her back.
