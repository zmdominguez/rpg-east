# Morning Decision — One Road Out of Town

**Time:** Dawn, day after arrival
**Location:** [[Thornhaven]] — wherever the party slept

## The State of Play

By morning, three factions are waiting on an answer:

| Faction | Contact | Where | Deadline |
|---------|---------|-------|----------|
| [[Cross Bones Inc]] | [[Mills]] | [[Cross Bones Pub]], corner table | Dawn — she's already there |
| [[Scarsith Temple]] | [[Nell]] | Temple, open until midday | Soft — but she notes if ignored |
| [[Owl Cult]] | [[Gravy]] | [[Halfheight Forest]] edge, at next dusk | Hard — one chance only |

## Why They Can Only Pick One

Thornhaven is small. Everyone watches the road.

- If the party rides out with [[Mills]] toward [[Aussen]], the temple's acolytes see them go. [[Nell]] marks them as Cross Bones-aligned and doesn't extend trust again — not soon.
- If the party walks the north road at dusk to meet [[Gravy]], the city gate guards note it. [[Eman]]'s office eventually hears. The Owl Cult connection is the one that would genuinely alarm her.
- If the party commits to the eastern farms census for [[Nell]], they're visibly working with the church. [[Mills]] notices. She won't be cold about it — but she'll find someone else for the ruins, and that door is harder to reopen later.

Attempting to split the party across two paths is possible — but each faction notices the party only sent part of themselves, and trust is earned by being chosen, not hedged.

## Running the Scene

Let the party discuss. Don't push. The pressure is structural.

If they're stuck, have each NPC make one more small move:

- A guard mentions in passing that [[Mills]] is already at the pub and ordered breakfast — she's clearly settling in to wait.
- A temple acolyte delivers a brief written note from [[Nell]]: *"Whenever you're ready."* No pressure in the words. Pressure in the timing.
- The owl feather is still on Selune's windowsill. It won't be there tomorrow.

## What Happens After the Choice

Whichever path they choose, mark it clearly. When the session ends, the party should know:

- Where they're going
- Who they're working with
- What the other factions will remember

The other two paths don't disappear — they cool. Future sessions may reopen them, but the relationships will be different.
