# The Scarsith Temple — Nell's Pitch

**Time:** Afternoon (if they go after receiving the midday invitation)
**Location:** [[Scarsith Temple]]
**Key NPCs:** [[Nell]], [[Brother Toben]]

## Getting There

At midday — wherever the party is — a thin acolyte in Scarsith grey finds them. The note is sealed with the temple's rat-skull mark.

> *"The High Priestess of Scarsith invites you to share a meal at the Temple. Scarsith provides for those who help others find what they need."*

The acolyte does not wait for an answer and does not take one.

**If the party ignores it:** The invitation doesn't come again. Nell notes it. She's patient but not desperate — she won't chase.

## The Temple

The [[Scarsith Temple]] sits on the outskirts of Thornhaven, built during the Quiet Year. Inside: plain stone, low ceilings, rows of benches worn smooth by years of use. It smells of cheap tallow candles and bread from the kitchen. People come here because they have nowhere else to go.

[[Nell]] receives the party in a plain room with a plain table. The meal is modest — bread, broth, roasted vegetables — and genuinely good.

## Nell's Pitch

She doesn't rush. She talks about the lower districts, the families the church feeds, the children who arrive at the temple steps before dawn because it's the only door that opens for them. She believes all of it. It's a long setup.

Then, at the end:

> *"I need someone I can trust to carry a message to the eastern farms. A census — who needs what, how much. Nothing illegal. But [[Eman]]'s office has taken to watching our couriers, and I cannot afford this particular message to go astray."*

**What she's really doing:** This is a trust test. The census mission is real and benign. She wants to know if the party can be discreet before she asks them for something harder.

**If asked why she can't send an acolyte:**
> *"I can. I'm asking you because Eman's people don't know your faces yet. That changes the longer you stay."*

**If the party mentions the blood root garden:** Nell's face changes. She knows blood root — the church uses small amounts medicinally through the [[Apothecary]] and [[Naven]]. A whole intact garden is something different. She asks quiet questions. She writes nothing down. She will speak to [[Naven]] privately after the party leaves.

## The Path This Opens

Leave [[Thornhaven]], travel east to the farms, carry the census request, return with information. Two to three days. No combat planned. Church trust gained. [[Eman]]'s office eventually notices who the party is keeping company with.

## Brother Toben — Selune's Thread

If [[Selune]] is present, an older priest sits in the corner of the room throughout the meal. He doesn't speak. He watches.

[[Brother Toben]] is one of the oldest members of the Scarsith congregation — slow-moving, weathered, rarely seen outside the temple's inner rooms. He has no obvious reason to be at this particular meal.

After it ends, he approaches Selune quietly, away from the others.

> *"The silver eclipse. Where did you see it?"*

He waits. He is not threatening. He has the posture of someone who has been waiting a very long time for exactly this moment.
