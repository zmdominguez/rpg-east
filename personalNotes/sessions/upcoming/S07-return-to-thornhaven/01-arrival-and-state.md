# Arrival & State of Thornhaven

## How the City Feels

Thornhaven has not changed — but the party has. Read the city differently this time:
- The guards at the gate recognize the party and wave them through with a nod. Familiar faces now.
- The Fat Pig Tavern has a new chalk sign: *"No tabs for Cloudbreak travelers"* — someone didn't pay on their way out.
- The Scarsith Temple's morning bell rings twice — a community announcement signal.
- The Cross Bones Pub is unusually quiet at this hour. Someone told the staff not to talk about the mountains.

## What People Have Heard

The party was hired by Roderick Ashford to find his daughter. That's public. What isn't:

| Rumor | Who Believes It | Truth |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| "Pipe Ashford died in the mountains" | Common folk, Roderick's neighbors | False |
| "Cross Bones had a job go badly sideways" | Anyone at the Pub | True |
| "Something attacked the Rootmother's Garden" | No one yet | True |
| "The Mayor hired Cross Bones Inc" | Nobody | True |

## Eman's Messenger

Within **one hour of the party entering the city**, a city guard in a clean dress uniform approaches them — wherever they are.

> *"The Mayor extends her compliments and requests the pleasure of your company at Town Hall, at your earliest convenience. She is aware you have only just returned."*

The tone is polite. The timing is not coincidental. Eman has eyes on the gate.

**If the party ignores the first message:** A second guard arrives within the hour. Slightly less formal.

> *"The Mayor asks again."*

**If ignored a second time:** Eman comes herself, with two guards. She's still smiling.
