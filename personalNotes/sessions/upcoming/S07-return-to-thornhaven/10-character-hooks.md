# Character Hooks — Backstory Threads

## Overview

The party has been in Thornhaven long enough to be recognized. Now the city starts recognizing them back — in personal ways.

Each hook below is an introduction, not a resolution. Plant one or two seeds this session. Let them grow.

**Rule of thumb:** Introduce at most two this session. Let them land before adding more.

---

## Aeris Whisperwind — The Familiar Stranger

**Thread:** Aeris "sees an unknown character from her childhood in the city."

**The hook:** On the first evening back — at the Cross Bones Pub during her usual set, or passing near The Fat Pig Tavern — Aeris spots someone in a crowd. She has seen this face before. Not a name, not a full memory — a face she places at Elarion's high court. A face that was there on the night Thalen performed his last private show.

The person notices her staring. They don't approach. They leave.

**Who is this person? (DM to choose):**
- A low-ranking court attendant who witnessed what happened to Thalen and fled Elarion afterward
- A spy sent from Elarion to locate Aeris — reasons unclear
- Someone who knew Thalen personally, in Thornhaven for their own unrelated purpose

**How to play it:** Don't resolve this session. Just let Aeris see the face. If she chases — the person is gone by the time she clears the crowd. A thread, not an answer.

## Kass — The Falcon's Detour

**Thread:** Kass's falcon can deliver mail to major city buildings. His backstory is not yet fully on paper — this hook starts building it.

**The hook:** While the party is in town, Kass's falcon goes out (or he sends it on a routine task). When it returns, it's carrying something it wasn't sent to collect: a small sealed note, bearing a wax seal Kass recognizes. Not Thornhaven's seal. Something personal — from Kass's past.

**The note (contents DM decides):**
- A warning — *"Don't trust the one who wears green in the market."*
- A demand — *"Meet me at the old gate at midnight. Come alone."*
- A name and nothing else — someone Kass hoped not to see again.

The falcon brought it without being asked. It watched Kass read it. It's waiting.

**What this opens:** Someone from Kass's past is in Thornhaven — or knows he is. Start here. Build backward.

## Mirr — The Collector's Daughter

**Thread:** Mirr's parents deal in rare artifacts. She travels to source things for resale or personal study.

**The hook:** In Thornhaven's morning market, Mirr spots a stall selling small, clearly old objects — ceramics and metalwork she immediately reads as Thelasian in origin. The stall owner is nervous, pricing low, moving fast.

When Mirr examines the goods, she finds one piece with a maker's mark she recognizes — the stamp of [[The Sealed Workshop]], a building on the older edge of [[Aussen]] that her parents used to source from. The workshop was shut by regional authority approximately thirty years ago. The doors are chained and wax-sealed.

**The stall owner, if pressed:** They bought it off a man passing through from the direction of the ruins near [[Aussen]]. He sold fast and left. He seemed scared.

**What this opens:** The goods are fresh — clean clay in the grooves, not decades of dust. Someone is inside [[The Sealed Workshop]] and removing stock. This is not excavation runoff from [[Dr Clair Bucklebub]]'s dig — it's a separate problem at the same location.

**Deeper thread ([[Library]] check with [[Anni]], DC 14 History):** The closure record is administrative with no stated reason. The workshop wasn't destroyed — it was deliberately and quietly sealed. The ledger inside lists Mirr's parents as regular buyers across fifteen years. They may know more than they've said.

## Selune — The Priest Who Waited

**Thread:** Selune's origins — cold hands, chanting voices, a silver eclipse carved in stone. Found at the roots of a white oak by the Circle of the Moon.

**The hook:** At or near the [[Scarsith Temple]] — whether Selune visits with the party or passes by alone — an older priest notices her. Not Nell. A quieter figure, weathered and slow-moving, who watches Selune across the room with unsettling patience.

After the others move on, he approaches her alone.

> *"The silver eclipse. Where did you see it?"*

He waits. He is not threatening. He has the posture of someone who has been waiting a very long time for exactly this moment.

**What he knows:** The silver eclipse symbol predates Thornhaven's founding. He found it in old records at the Library — and once, years ago, on a young woman he knew briefly. A woman connected to a druidic circle older than the Owl Cult.

**What this opens:** Selune's origin is not randomness — it's a lineage. The cult that "shaped her for darkness" had a specific purpose tied to something in this region. The priest is a lead, not a resolution. He doesn't have the full answer. He's just the person who recognized the question.

**DM note:** This hooks into Selune's backstory and into the Owl Cult's deeper history. Take it slowly — it should feel like something has been waiting, not like a quest giver handing over a map.
