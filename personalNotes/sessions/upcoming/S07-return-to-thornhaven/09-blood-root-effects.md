# Blood Root — Long-Term Effects

## What Blood Root Actually Does

Blood root is an ancient plant connected to the age of roots — a time when the boundary between mortal life and the deep earth was thin. In small doses it amplifies magic. In ritual quantities, it binds a soul to the root network, slowly weaving the bearer into the world's oldest living system.

The process is gradual. Not painful. Not evil. But not fully reversible.

**Three stages of progression:**
1. **Stirring** — Subtle. Mostly ignored. (S07–S08)
2. **Speaking** — The roots start to communicate back. (S09–S11)
3. **Weaving** — The bearer begins to visibly change. (S12+)

---

## Universal Effects (All Four)

Starting S07, all party members experience:
- Dreams of darkness and warmth — like being underground but not trapped. Pressure. The sound of something growing.
- Plants in the same room lean faintly toward them, imperceptibly at first.
- After a short rest taken outdoors, they wake feeling unnaturally restored — like the ground gave something back.

---

## Per Character — Stirring Stage (S07–S08)

### [[Aeris Whisperwind]]
*Single exposure. Bard. Attuned to the Whisperwind Quill.*

When Aeris plays music, tiny flowers and shoots occasionally push through cracks in nearby stone or wood. Small. Easy to dismiss as coincidence. Other people notice before she does.

**Once per session (DM triggers):** A song she plays carries an emotion she didn't consciously put into it — something she was suppressing. The plants amplify it. The audience feels it clearly.

**Quirk:** The Whisperwind Quill occasionally writes a few words she didn't intend — small, root-like script at the margins of her notes. The words are always true. Never comfortable.

---

### [[Kass]]
*Double exposure. Attuned to the Stoneheart Gauntlet.*

Kass's effects are already ahead of the others.

When he grips something tightly — a wall, a weapon, the ground — faint root-pattern veins appear under his skin, visible for just a moment before fading. Not painful. Not controllable.

**Once per session:** Nearby plants lean independently toward him, a slow visible lean. If he's in danger and panicking, small roots crack through the nearest organic surface without his intention — a door, a wooden floor, a tree nearby.

**Quirk:** His falcon has become strongly and strangely attached. It refuses to deliver messages to locations it "dislikes" — returning early from some tasks without explanation. The bird is reacting to the blood root in Kass's blood. It stays close.

---

### [[Mirr]]
*Single exposure. Warlock/Fighter. Attuned to the Pactbreaker Shard.*

The blood root is in tension with Mirr's warlock pact. Her patron has noticed. The Pactbreaker Shard goes cold for no visible reason — sometimes mid-conversation, not near anything extraplanar.

**Once per session:** When Mirr uses a warlock spell, the impact residue leaves faint root-like burn marks on the surface hit — beautiful, almost decorative, but not something she did intentionally.

**Quirk:** The tattoo on Mirr's arm occasionally shows a tiny vine growing around the letters for a few seconds after she uses warlock magic. Only visible to her — and anyone close enough to notice.

---

### [[Selune]]
*Single exposure. Druid. Attuned to the Moonpetal Circlet.*

As a druid, Selune processes blood root more naturally than the others — but more naturally doesn't mean safely.

**Passive (always on from S07):** Within 30 feet, Selune instinctively senses the presence of large root systems — an ambient awareness, like knowing there's water nearby. She doesn't understand what this is yet. She just knows where the ground is deep.

**Once per session:** When she Wild Shapes, the animal form carries faint root-pattern markings in its fur or skin. The forms are also slightly larger than expected for the species.

**Quirk:** Animals that were already strange around Selune are stranger now. They don't approach — they wait. As if deferring to her, or waiting for instruction.

| Stage | Others | Kass |

| Stirring | S07–S08 | Already started at S07 |
| Speaking | S09–S11 | S08–S09 |

He may hit visible change before the party understands what's happening to any of them. Consider signaling this to Marco so he can lean into it — the double exposure was his choice (stealing blood root in S05).

## What Cures It

The Owl Cult knows. They won't share for free.

Gravy has slowed the progression in past druids who were overexposed — but slowing is not the same as reversing. At Stage 3 (Weaving), reversal requires significant cost. What that cost is, the party doesn't know yet.
