# Exhaustion

## Overview

Exhaustion represents the physical and mental toll of pushing beyond your limits. It is not only a punishment — it is a **tactical resource**. Players can voluntarily accept exhaustion to gain power in desperate moments, but must manage it carefully or risk collapsing at the worst possible time.

Exhaustion has **5 levels**. At Level 5, the character collapses and cannot continue until they complete a full Long Rest.

## Exhaustion Levels

Each level of exhaustion applies a cumulative penalty to **all d20 rolls** (attack rolls, ability checks, saving throws) and to your **spell save DC**.

| Level | Penalty                                                                            |
| ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | −1 to all rolls, spell save DC or any value                                        |
| 2     | −2 to all rolls, spell save DC or any value                                        |
| 3     | −3 to all rolls, spell save DC or any value                                        |
| 4     | −4 to all rolls, spell save DC or any value                                        |
| 5     | **Unconscious** — cannot be roused until completing a Long Rest in safe conditions |

*The penalty equals the exhaustion level. A character at Level 3 has a −3 penalty to everything.*

## Gaining Exhaustion

### Environmental / Involuntary

- **Harsh conditions:** Failing a Constitution saving throw during extreme heat, cold, forced march, or starvation (DC set by DM based on severity)
- **Sleep deprivation:** After 24 hours without sleep, gain 1 level. Gain 1 additional level for each subsequent day without a Long Rest
- **Near death:** Failing two or more death saving throws in a single encounter
- **Revived from 0 HP:** Gain 1 level each time you are brought back from unconsciousness in combat

### Voluntary — Push Mechanics

Players may **choose** to accept exhaustion in exchange for immediate power. These decisions should feel weighty.

---

**Arcane Push**

When you have no remaining spell slots of the required level, you may still cast a spell by gaining exhaustion levels equal to the spell's level.

> *Example: You have no 2nd-level slots. You cast Misty Step and gain 2 levels of exhaustion.*

You must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 10 + spell level). On a failure, you also lose concentration on any active spell.

---

**Physical Push**

As a Bonus Action, gain 1 level of exhaustion to take an additional Action this turn. That extra Action cannot itself trigger another Push.

---

**Desperate Defense**

When you would be reduced to 0 HP, you may choose to gain 2 levels of exhaustion to remain at 1 HP instead. Usable once per Long Rest.

---

## Recovery

| Method | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Long Rest (basic) | Remove 1 level of exhaustion |
| Long Rest (with adequate food and water) | Remove 2 levels of exhaustion |
| *Greater Restoration* or equivalent magic | Remove 1 level of exhaustion |
| Waking from Level 5 | Wake at Level 3 after a full uninterrupted Long Rest |

### Recovering from Level 5

A character who reaches Level 5 collapses and is unconscious. They cannot be roused by mundane means. Once they complete a full, uninterrupted Long Rest in a reasonably safe environment, they wake at **Level 3** — still impaired, but functional.

## Tactical Decisions

The exhaustion system is designed to make rest a real strategic choice, not a mechanical obligation:

- **When to sleep:** Pushing on costs exhaustion levels; stopping costs time. Dungeons don't pause.
- **Arcane Push:** Out of spells? You can still cast — but each level costs you. A 5th-level spell (5 exhaustion) is immediately incapacitating. Low-level spells are manageable if you're careful.
- **Physical Push:** Need one more action at a critical moment? Pay with fatigue.
- **Risk stacking:** Exhaustion doesn't reset quickly. Two hard fights with no rest will show.
