# Actions in a Turn

## Overview

Your turn has three action slots, each with a **Rank** that reflects its "power level." You may use any slot for any activity, but using a lower-ranked slot for a higher-tier activity applies a **penalty**.

| Slot | Rank | Default Use |
|------|------|-------------|
| Action | 1 — Highest | Attack, full spell, major activity |
| Movement | 2 — Middle | Move up to your speed |
| Bonus Action | 3 — Lowest | Minor abilities, off-hand attacks |

## Core Rule: Repurposing Slots

### Penalty Table

| Using Slot | For Activity | Penalty |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| Movement (Rank 2) | Action-level activity | Disadvantage on the roll |
| Bonus (Rank 3) | Action-level activity | Disadvantage + no ability modifier to damage |
| Bonus (Rank 3) | Movement-level activity | Move up to half your speed |

### Examples

**You attack with your Action, then want to attack again using your Movement:**
→ Disadvantage on the second attack roll.

**You use your Bonus Action to attack (no class feature grants this):**
→ Disadvantage and your STR/DEX modifier does not apply to damage.

**You already moved and want to use your Bonus Action to move further:**
→ You move up to half your normal speed.

**You try to cast a spell using your Movement:**
→ Disadvantage on the spell attack roll (if any); no ability modifier to damage for spells that use one.

## Spell Slot Limit

**You may only expend one spell slot per turn**, regardless of features, reactions, or other mechanics that might otherwise allow additional spellcasting. Free cantrips and abilities that do not consume a slot are unaffected.

## Exceptions

**Class Features:** Abilities that explicitly grant a Bonus Action use — Cunning Action, Two-Weapon Fighting's off-hand attack, Bonus Action spells from subclass features — are **not penalized**. They are designed for that slot.

**Extra Attack:** Multiple attacks granted by the Extra Attack class feature are all part of one Action. No penalty applies between them.

**Dash:** Using your Bonus Action to Dash moves you up to half your speed (same cap as the Bonus→Movement penalty). Using your Movement to Dash still doubles your full speed.

## Design Intent

This system keeps the three-slot structure intact but rewards using each slot for its intended role. It creates real costs for mixing action types — a fighter who wants three attacks in a round can have them, but the third carries compounding penalties. Think before you act.
