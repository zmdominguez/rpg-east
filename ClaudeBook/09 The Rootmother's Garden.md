# Chapter Six: The Rootmother's Garden

---

Dawn broke over the [[Veiled Peaks]] to silence. [[Venn]] was gone — no tracks, no note, no sign he had ever sheltered there at all. The Waterkeeper had slipped away as quietly as the mist.

They moved on.

The six-legged wolf came out of the white nothing without warning. [[Kass]] was somewhere behind them, lost in the fog, so [[Mirr]], [[Selune]], and [[Aeris Whisperwind]] held it alone. Kass found them by sound — arriving mid-fight with unhurried calm, as though she had never been worried. The wolf died. They pressed deeper into the mist.

It was Kass who spotted the pack next — dark shapes moving in exactly the direction they were heading. Selune stopped and studied the mountain. She found an owl perched on a grey stone, tearing at a lizard. A desert lizard. Something with no right to exist in this snow.

She called it an omen. They followed the owl.

It led them to [[Cloudbreak Pass]] — not a portal, though [[Mirr]] had hoped for one, but something stranger: a natural wall where cold air pressed against desert heat, snow and scrubland existing side by side without explanation. They stepped through the threshold, and the temperature hit like opening an oven door. The sun above the plateau was relentless, and it cost them.

---

Mirr remembered something [[Venn]] had said about an arch. Kass spotted it from a distance, half-buried in pale stone. Beyond it lay the Rootmother's Garden — and beyond that: [[Pipe Ashford]].

She was alive. Older, quieter, and unsurprised to see them.

After a brief rest, the questions came. Would she return to [[Thornhaven]]? No. The garden needed a guardian. Her father's grief moved her — she couldn't hide that — but her answer held. [[Eman]]'s love, she said, was never love. It was appetite. Eman wanted power, and [[Blood root|Blood Root]] was the path to it.

Pipe spoke of Blood Root the way a priest speaks of something sacred and dangerous: it healed the land, not people, and in the wrong hands it amplified whatever darkness was already there. She held a book containing everything she had learned. She did not offer it.

While [[Selune]] listened with growing conviction and Mirr pushed back, Kass excused herself for a moment of privacy. She returned lighter on her feet, her pockets heavier.

Selune accepted a letter from Pipe — addressed to [[Roderick Ashford]]. The party made camp for the night.

---

Mirr's alarm trap woke them before dawn.

[[Cross Bones Inc]] had found the garden. They moved fast: Pipe knocked unconscious before anyone could call out, tents burning orange in the dark. Then the earth split. The stone at the garden's centre cracked and rose — the Root Guardian, ancient and enormous, answering for its fallen mistress.

And into the chaos the mercenaries drove something else: a Desert Yeti, baited off the plateau and aimed like a weapon into the heart of everything Pipe had sworn to protect.

The garden was no longer a sanctuary.
